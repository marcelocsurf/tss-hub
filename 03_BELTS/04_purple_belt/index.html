Welcome to Purple Belt

You ride complete waves. You link sections. You execute named expressions with consistency. You own your sessions. Now everything commits.

Purple Belt is not about learning a new sequence. The sequence you built through White, Yellow, and Blue Belt stays — it is the same architecture. What changes is the depth of commitment, the power of execution, the variety of expression, and the emergence of your identity as a surfer within that sequence.

The Core Shift at Purple Belt

The Shift

Blue Belt: "Can you surf a complete wave consistently?" Purple Belt: "What kind of surfer are you becoming?"

This belt builds three things:

  • Commitment — Full commitment on both frontside and backside. No hesitation at the point of release.
  • Variety — 10+ named expressions explored within the same recurring sequence architecture.
  • Identity — An emerging personal style. The surfer begins to answer: "Who am I on a wave?"
Internal Micro-Progression

#14 Backside Commitment → #15 Frontside Commitment → #16 Frontside Variety → #17 Backside Variety → Level Readiness gate to Brown Belt.

Doctrinal Rule

The base sequence architecture (pop-up → posture → board connection → bottom turn → projection → rail change → expression → recovery) does not change. What changes is the commitment, the power, the variety of expression at each step, and the combinations within a single wave.

Foundation — Mental Pillar

ONE WAVE

One Wave is the TSS mental framework (Pillar 4: Mental). At Purple Belt, it becomes the operating system for commitment, presence, and intentional surfing.

One Wave — As Commitment Principle

Every wave is one wave. You do not carry the last wave into this one. You do not plan the next one yet. Full commitment means full presence. If you are thinking about the wave you missed, you are not committed to the wave you are on.

One Wave — As Tactical Frame

Before you paddle, you know your intent: which expression, which section, which commitment level. One wave, one mission. This is not about surfing more waves — it is about surfing each wave with full intention.

One Wave — As Creative Confidence

Trying new expressions means accepting failure. One Wave frees you: this wave is one attempt. If it fails, it fails completely. The next one is a fresh start. This is how personal style develops — through committed attempts, not cautious half-measures.

One Wave — As Identity Formation

Who are you on this wave? Not who do you want to be. Not who are you copying. What feels natural, powerful, and true when you commit fully to this one wave? Purple Belt is where you discover this.

Coach Application of One Wave

As coaching cue: Before water entry — "What is your one mission for this wave?" After a failed attempt — "Next wave. Clean slate. Same commitment."

As session design principle: Each session has a One Wave focus. Not "surf well" but "this session, every wave gets a committed deep bottom turn into projection." Specific, expression-focused, present.

As debrief tool: "On which wave did you commit fully? What happened on that wave vs. the ones where you held back?"

Block 0 — Surf Literacy

Safety & Calculated Risk

Purple Belt introduces risk as a skill. Committed maneuvers carry higher consequences. Safety evolves — it does not decrease.

Conditions & Limits

  • Wave size: Up to 6 feet (green waves) confidently. Overhead (6+) optional with coach assessment and demonstrated readiness at 4–6 foot range.
  • Equipment: Performance board appropriate to conditions and surfer size. Active participation in board selection. Leash checked every session.
  • Wipeout management: Head protection on every fall — non-negotiable. Board awareness during dynamic wipeouts. Calm during turbulence. Surface, check surroundings, then retrieve board.
  • Self-regulation: Fully autonomous. Recognizing fatigue, managing adrenaline after failed attempts, choosing to end a session when conditions or performance deteriorate.

Coach — Supervision Protocol

Role: Consultant, not director. Ratio: 1:8–12 depending on conditions. Strategic feedback only. Must be available for conditions assessment if overhead waves are attempted.

Calculated Risk Rule

Before every committed attempt, three questions: Is this section appropriate for this maneuver at my level? What happens if it fails — is the landing zone safe? Am I physically and mentally ready for the consequence? If any answer is uncertain, simplify or exit.

Blocks 1–3 — Entry (Autonomous)

Session Design & Venue Ownership

At Purple Belt, the surfer designs the session. The coach confirms or adjusts. The surfer owns the entire session cycle.

Session Structure

  • Session Design (surfer-led): ~10 min — Present session plan: One Wave focus, wave criteria, maneuver intent, risk assessment.
  • Warm-Up: 10–15 min — High-intensity dynamic mobility, explosive pop-up drills, rotation power.
  • Land / Skate Drills: 10–15 min — Maneuver simulation, commitment practice at speed.
  • Venue Analysis (surfer-led): ~5 min — Fully autonomous. Identify sections, lineup strategy, session zones.
  • Water Session: 90–120 min — Commitment and variety missions. Coach provides strategic feedback only.
  • Video Analysis: 15–20 min — Surfer reviews own footage first. Identifies commitment level, section selection, recovery quality. Coach adds one observation.
  • Session Debrief (surfer-led): 5–10 min — What worked, what needs adjustment, next-session priority. Coach confirms.

Key Change from Blue Belt

The surfer designs the session plan. Debrief starts and ends with the surfer. Coach intervention during water session is minimal and strategic.

If the surfer cannot design a session independently, they are not ready for Purple Belt.

Block 4 — Power Posture

Posture · Board Connection · Speed

Block 4 is the foundation and the recovery point. At Purple Belt, posture is dynamic, board connection is instinctive, and speed generation is automatic.

P·R·C·H at Block 4

  • P — Posture: Dynamic, pulsing with the wave. Not static — it loads and unloads as power tools. Back straight, head up, center of gravity responsive.
  • C — Compression: Used actively for speed generation. Compress to load energy, extend to release it. At Purple Belt, compression reaches maximum depth before every committed action.

Board Connection

The board is an extension of your body. At Purple Belt, this is not a concept — it is a physical reality you feel through your feet. You feel the rail, the flex, the feedback. You adjust mid-maneuver based on what the board tells you.

Feet Position: Position 1 (neutral, for speed and wave-face riding) and Position 2 (engaged, back foot toward tail for aggressive maneuvers) are used intentionally based on maneuver intent. In more radical expressions, the rear foot may shift further back as part of the setup — this is deliberate, not accidental.

Coach Diagnostic — Block 4

If the surfer loses speed after a committed maneuver:

  • Is the surfer returning to a low, centered base after the action?
  • Is impulse (compression/extension for speed) immediate after recovery?
  • Is the board connection intact — or did the surfer disconnect during the maneuver?

"The maneuver is not complete until speed is recovered and posture is reset."

Block 5 — Deep Bottom Turn

Deep BT · Hold · Rail Engagement

The deep bottom turn is the foundation of every committed Purple Belt expression. Full rail engagement, maximum compression, held longer than feels natural.

P·R·C·H at Block 5

  • R — Rotation: The engine of the turn. Eyes lead → neck → torso → obliques → hips → ankles. Full kinetic chain. At Purple Belt, rotation is powerful and committed — not tentative.
  • C — Compression: Maximum depth before release. Deeper compression = more powerful projection. The surfer who skips compression produces flat turns.
  • H — Hold: Sustain the rail engagement longer than feels natural. Hold through the apex, then release. This is what separates rushed surfing from intentional surfing.
Commitment Rule

A fully committed medium bottom turn is worth more than a tentative deep one. If the bottom turn is flat, the maneuver above it will be flat. If the bottom turn is deep and committed, the projection will carry the power you need.

Coach Diagnostic — Block 5

When a Purple Belt surfer's maneuver lacks power, trace it back to the bottom turn:

  • Was the compression deep enough?
  • Did the rotation complete through the full kinetic chain?
  • Was the hold long enough before release?
  • Was the rail locked through the turn — or did the surfer pull back?

The block before the failure is usually where the real problem lives.

Block 6 — Projection & Direction

Projection (Frontside) · Choke (Backside)

Block 6 is where the energy from the bottom turn is directed toward the target on the wave face. Frontside and backside use different mechanics to achieve the same function: direction and force.

Frontside: Projection

Projection carries the surfer from the bottom turn toward the target section. The direction is set by the eyes and chest. At Purple Belt, projection carries more force because the bottom turn was deeper and the hold was longer. You project through critical sections, not around them.

Backside: Choke

Choke is the backside equivalent of projection. It organizes direction, extension, and intention on the backside.

The analogy: imagine executing a rear naked choke (mata león) in jiu-jitsu. The chest comes forward, the arms wrap, the legs extend. In surfing, this means:

  • Chest and eyes point toward the pocket or toward where you want to go
  • Legs extend, pushing through the board
  • The body opens in the direction of the intended projection

Choke is not a maneuver — it is a directional mechanism. It sets up everything that follows on the backside: the Tapaloco, the reentry, the expression. Without Choke, backside surfing lacks direction.

Coach Diagnostic — Block 6

Frontside: When a maneuver fails despite good body mechanics — was the projection directed to a clear target, or was the surfer drifting? Was the section appropriate for that expression?

Backside: When backside surfing lacks direction — is the surfer doing Choke? Is the chest opening toward the pocket? Are the legs extending? If Choke is missing, the backside sequence has no compass.

Step 7 — La Cruz (Rail Change)

La Cruz is the moment of rail change on frontside. It is not a maneuver name — it is a functional action within the sequence. At the point where the surfer needs to change direction from the bottom turn projection to the maneuver target:

  • The oblique engages — this drives the directional change
  • The eyes shift to the new target
  • The rail switches from inside to outside (or vice versa)

La Cruz is a critical transition. It connects the energy of the bottom turn and projection to the maneuver expression. Without a clean Cruz, the energy dissipates before the action.

Step 8 — Maneuver Expression (Frontside)

This is where variety lives. The expression changes — the architecture before and after it stays the same. At Purple Belt, frontside expressions include:

  • Cruz Reentry — fluid section crossing with connected transitions
  • Tango — creative expression within the rail change
  • Asking God / Pidiendo a Dios (Felipe Toledo) — expressive upper-body commitment
  • Praying — arms and torso engaged forward in full commitment
  • Snap, Floater, Tail Slide, Layback — explored with commitment (#16)

The expression is the variable. The sequence is the constant.

Step 9 — Grenade (Aggressive Recovery)

Grenade is not just "return to posture." It is an aggressive, intentional closure of the action that restores structure with force.

  • It can come directly from La Cruz or from the maneuver expression
  • It is an explosive compression back to power posture
  • It recovers speed, resets the base, and prepares the next cycle of the Infinite Circle

Grenade is what separates a single-action ride from a linked, multi-expression wave. Without Grenade, the surfer completes one action and stalls. With Grenade, the surfer completes one action and immediately reloads for the next.

Coach Note — Frontside Architecture

Present this to the surfer as a recurring pattern, not a one-time checklist. On a single wave at Purple Belt, steps 5–9 may repeat 3–5 times with different expressions each cycle. The architecture is the skeleton. The expressions are the personality.

When a frontside ride breaks down, trace back through the sequence. The failure point is usually 1–2 steps before the visible problem.

Step 6 — Choke (Backside Projection)

Already defined in Block 6. On the backside, Choke is the directional mechanism: chest and eyes point toward the target, legs extend through the board. It sets up everything that follows.

Step 7 — Tapaloco (Backside Rail Change)

Tapaloco is the backside equivalent of La Cruz. It is the moment where the rail changes direction on the backside. This is not just a maneuver name — it is a highly repeated functional action within backside surfing.

  • The rail switches at the direction set by Choke
  • The body transitions from the compression of the bottom turn to the release toward the expression
  • Tapaloco can lead to multiple different expressions — reentry, carve, floater, tail slide

Tapaloco is the gateway action. What comes after it defines the expression. Tapaloco itself defines the rail transition.

Step 8 — Maneuver Expression (Backside)

Backside expressions at Purple Belt include:

  • Tapaloco Reentry — committed backside reentry from deep BT, more vertical than Blue Belt
  • Backside Carve — sustained rail through an open section
  • Backside Floater — over crumbling lip sections
  • Tail Slide (BS) — explored with commitment
  • Gallina Muerta — advanced backside expression (#17)

Step 9 — Codazo + Touch the Board (Backside Recovery)

This is the backside equivalent of Grenade. After Tapaloco and the expression, the way to close the action and recycle the sequence is through Codazo + Touch the Board.

  • Codazo (Elbow): The elbow drives additional rotation and torque, extending the action's power through the close
  • Touch the Board: After the Codazo, the hand touches the board — goofy touches with the right hand, regular with the left
  • Function: Touching the board lowers the hips, drops the center of gravity, and resets the surfer into a strong power posture

This is not a style move. It is a functional closure mechanism: it recovers balance, resets posture, and prepares the next cycle of the Infinite Circle on the backside.

Coach Note — Backside Parity

Purple Belt demands that the surfer's weaker side develops. Frontside-only expression is incomplete. Track the FS/BS ratio across sessions. If the surfer avoids backside consistently, assign dedicated backside sessions.

When a backside ride lacks direction, check Choke first. When a backside ride lacks power at the close, check Codazo + Touch the Board.

Block 7 — Expression

Maneuver Variety (Sequences #16–#17)

The surfer's repertoire expands significantly within the existing sequence architecture. Not all are required for promotion — they are explored with commitment.

Variety as Intelligence

Attempting different maneuvers on different waves is not random experimentation. It is tactical intelligence: reading the wave section and choosing the expression that fits. A surfer who does the same thing on every wave is not reading the wave — they are repeating a pattern.

Frontside Variety (#16)

All expressions follow the same frontside architecture (BT → Projection → La Cruz → Expression → Grenade). What varies is the action at step 8:

  • Reentry — vertical approach to the lip from committed projection
  • Snap — sharp, explosive turn at the top of the wave face
  • Floater — over crumbling lips and closeout sections
  • Tail Slide — controlled tail release through the turn
  • Tango, Asking God, Layback, Sandwich, Ollie, Chop Pop — creative expressions explored

The surfer discovers which expressions fit their body, their style, and the waves they ride.

Backside Variety (#17)

All expressions follow the same backside architecture (BT → Choke → Tapaloco → Expression → Codazo + Touch the Board). What varies is the action at step 8:

  • Tapaloco Reentry — deepened, more vertical
  • Backside Floater — over crumbling sections
  • Backside Tail Slide — explored on both sides
  • Gallina Muerta — advanced backside creative expression

Maneuver-Wave Matching

  • Critical section (steep, powerful pocket) → committed maneuver: deep BT, snap, reentry
  • Open section (mellow, wide) → flow, linking, cutbacks, speed generation
  • Closeout section (breaks all at once) → floater or safe exit
  • Hollow section (barrel-like) → layback, tube attempt with caution

Choosing the wrong maneuver for the section wastes energy. Choosing the right one makes the wave do the work.

Frameworks

P·R·C·H & Three Circles at Purple Belt

The foundational language of the method. At Purple Belt, these operate as a fully integrated, real-time system. The surfer feels the interaction instinctively.

P
Posture
Dynamic. Loads and unloads as power tools. The base of all action.
R
Rotation
Powerful, committed. Full kinetic chain. The engine of expression.
C
Compression
Maximum depth before release. Deeper = more powerful projection.
H
Hold
Sustained through power sections. Do not release early. Hold through the apex.

Circle 1 — Body

Rotation is powerful and committed. Compression reaches maximum depth. Extension is explosive. Full kinetic chain engagement through every maneuver. Posture recovery after high-impact maneuvers is trained.

Diagnostic: When power is missing → check compression depth, rotation chain, hold duration.

Circle 2 — Board

Board as extension of the body. Rail engagement is strong and sustained. Feet Position 1 & 2 used intentionally. Touch the Board adds a new dimension of board interaction. Mid-maneuver adjustment based on board feedback.

Diagnostic: When turns lack commitment → check back foot engagement, rail lock, trust in board response.

Circle 3 — Wave

Reads 3–5 seconds ahead. Distinguishes critical, open, closeout, and hollow sections. Selects maneuver based on section. Aggressive pocket management — stays in the power zone by choice.

Diagnostic: When maneuver fails despite good mechanics → check section appropriateness, wave reading vs. memorized plan.

Key Shift from Blue Belt

At Blue, the loop was functional. At Purple, the loop is powerful. Compression is deeper, rotation is more committed, projection carries more force, rail changes (Cruz/Tapaloco) are cleaner, expressions are more varied, and recovery (Grenade/Codazo+Touch) is faster because the next cycle begins immediately.

The Infinite Circle is the coach's primary diagnostic tool during rides. When a surfer's execution breaks down, ask: which block broke? The block before the failure point is usually where the real correction belongs.

Practice

Water Missions

One Wave focus for each mission. Commitment, variety, and self-direction in green waves up to 6 feet.

Mission 01

Deep Bottom Turn (FS + BS)

  • Full rail, max compression, powerful projection. Hold through the apex.
  • Track FS/BS ratio. Do not avoid your weaker side.
Mission 02

Frontside Architecture: Full Pattern

  • Execute the full frontside pattern on every wave: BT → Projection → La Cruz → Expression → Grenade
  • Any expression — the goal is completing the architecture, not the specific trick
Mission 03

Backside Architecture: Full Pattern

  • BT → Choke → Tapaloco → Expression → Codazo + Touch the Board
  • Focus on Choke direction and Codazo closure quality
Mission 04

Tapaloco Reentry

  • Committed backside reentry from deep BT through Choke. Vertical approach, controlled landing, speed recovery.
Mission 05

Grenade Commitment

  • After every frontside expression, execute Grenade with explosive force back to posture. Not gentle. Aggressive closure and reload.
Mission 06

Maneuver Variety Session

  • 3+ different named expressions in one session, selected by wave section. Log which expressions were attempted and which succeeded.
Mission 07

Backside Variety Session

  • Entire session dedicated to backside. Do not switch to frontside even when easier waves appear.
Mission 08

Section Selection Drill

  • During each ride, identify section type before choosing expression. Post-session: did you match the maneuver to the section?
Mission 09

Personal Style Exploration (One Wave)

  • 3 waves focused on what feels most natural to you. No copying. After: what did my surfing look like? What expressions felt like me?
Practice

On-Land & Skate Drills

Commitment practice at speed. Land preparation is shorter but more intense than Blue Belt.

Skate

Deep Bottom Turn Simulation

  • Ramp or pump track. Compress deeply at the bottom, hold through the turn, extend explosively at the top. 5 FS, 5 BS.
Land

Commitment Drill — Point of No Return

  • Simulate BT into maneuver on flat ground. On coach's "GO!" — extend fully, rotate completely, project with max energy. No pull-back. 10 reps alternating FS/BS.
Land

Rotation Power Circuit

  • Power stance, resistance band at waist. FS rotation against resistance (10 reps), BS rotation (10 reps). Remove resistance — feel the speed difference. Add simulated BT before each. 3 sets.
Cognitive

Section Matching Visualization

  • 10 wave photos/clips paused at section reveal. Identify section type + name the expression + explain why. Goal: 8/10 correct.
Land

Session Design Drill

  • Written session plan: One Wave focus, wave criteria, maneuver intent, risk assessment. Coach reviews before water entry.
Coach Tools

Diagnostic & Common Corrections

Trace the failure to its block. Correct the block, not the symptom. The block before the failure is usually where the real problem lives.

Hesitation at Point of Release

✗ BT commits but surfer pulls back at the top. Fear of consequence. (C1 Body)
✓ "Trust the setup." If the BT was good, the maneuver will work. Commit. One Wave — this attempt only.

Flat Bottom Turns Despite Intent

✗ Body stays upright, rail not engaged. (C1 + C2)
✓ "Compress lower, hold longer." Deep BT on skate. Video review of compression depth.

No Direction on Backside

✗ Backside rides drift without purpose. No committed projection. (Block 6)
✓ Check Choke. Is the chest opening toward the pocket? Are legs extending? "Mata león — then go."

Same Expression Every Wave

✗ Defaults to comfort zone. Does not read sections. (C3 Wave)
✓ "Read the section, choose the expression." Assign maneuver variety missions.

Avoiding Backside

✗ Unconsciously selects only FS waves. (C3 + Mental)
✓ Dedicated BS sessions. Track FS/BS ratio. "Backside next. No exceptions."

Speed Dies After Expression

✗ Recovery incomplete. No impulse after release. (B4 + C1/C2)
✓ FS: "Grenade — explosive back to posture, then impulse." BS: "Codazo, touch the board, hips down, go."

Forcing Maneuvers on Wrong Sections

✗ Critical expression on open/weak section. (C3 Wave)
✓ "Match the maneuver to the wave." Post-session section selection review.

No Session Plan

✗ Enters water without goals. Sessions become reactive. (Mental)
✓ "Design before you paddle." Written plan required. One Wave focus.

Progression & Promotion

Binary checklist — every item must be met for promotion to Brown Belt (Pre-Elite). Evaluated across multiple sessions and conditions.

Progression Markers

Early Purple (#14–#15 Entry)

  • Bottom turns visibly deeper and more committed than Blue Belt
  • Backside shows intention and power, not just execution
  • Committed reentries (Tapaloco Reentry, Cruz Reentry) attempted most sessions
  • Session design attempted independently, even if imperfect

Mid Purple (#15–#16 Transition)

  • Projects through critical sections with visible power
  • New expressions attempted with commitment, not hesitation
  • FS/BS balance maintained without coach prompting
  • Self-correction in real-time during rides

Late Purple (#16–#17 / Approaching Promotion)

  • 4+ different expressions demonstrated across sessions
  • Recognizable personal style emerging
  • Full session ownership across multiple conditions
  • Risk assessment autonomous and accurate
  • Articulates development priorities and surfing identity

Promotion Criteria → Brown Belt (Pre-Elite)

Binary checklist. Every item met. Coach sign-off required.

Commitment & Power

  • Commits to critical takeoffs consistently
  • Projects through maneuvers with visible power
  • Deep bottom turns (FS+BS) with full rail engagement are the norm
  • Hold maintained through power sections before release

Maneuver Variety & Expression

  • Different maneuvers selected intentionally by wave section
  • 4+ named expressions demonstrated across sessions
  • Backside variety matches or approaches frontside in commitment
  • Emerging personal style identifiable

Sequence Architecture

  • Frontside architecture (BT → Projection → La Cruz → Expression → Grenade) executed as pattern
  • Backside architecture (BT → Choke → Tapaloco → Expression → Codazo+Touch) executed as pattern
  • Completes waves with multiple linked expressions as the norm
  • Recovers speed and posture after committed maneuvers (Grenade / Codazo+Touch)

Independence & Decision-Making

  • Designs and owns session goals independently
  • Reads wave sections at speed and selects appropriate expressions
  • Self-corrects in real-time during rides
  • Takes calculated risks appropriately

Physical & Mental

  • Sustains 90–120 min sessions with committed performance throughout
  • Composure in high-pressure situations and after failed attempts
  • Safety and risk assessment fully autonomous

Tracking Scales

  • Focus (1–5): Session mission adherence
  • Composure (1–5): Maintained across committed attempts and failures
  • Commitment (1–5): NEW. 1 = hesitant/pulls back. 5 = fully committed every attempt.
  • Variety (1–5): NEW. 1 = same expression every wave. 5 = adapts expression to section.
  • Autonomy (1–5): From Blue. 5 = fully self-directed, zero coach intervention.

Red Flags / Non-Promotion

  • Hesitates consistently at point of release
  • Avoids backside consistently
  • Only one or two expressions attempted
  • Cannot sustain session intensity
  • Still needs coach direction for session goals
  • Cannot assess risk accurately
  • No identifiable personal style emerging

What Comes Next: Brown Belt (Pre-Elite)

At Brown, commitment and variety become the raw material for competition-ready precision across all conditions and break types.

Brown asks: "Now that you know who you are as a surfer, can you perform under any conditions?"
