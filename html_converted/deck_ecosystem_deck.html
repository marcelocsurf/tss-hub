TOOL 01 — INITIAL ASSESSMENT

Every surfer starts with a diagnosis.

Not a guess.

  • The consultation is the entry point to TSS. Before any session, any mission, any plan —
  • we need to know exactly where the student is.

WHAT IT IS

  • A structured intake conversation between coach and student that maps current level, goals, physical background, learning history, and mental approach to training.

WHY IT EXISTS

  • Without diagnosis, coaching is guesswork. The assessment ensures every student enters the right level of the TSS progression — no skipping, no underestimating.

WHAT IT COVERS

  • Surf experience & frequency · Physical capacity · Mental approach (free surf vs. training mindset) · Goals (short / medium / long term) · Fears and blockers

OUTPUT

  • Student assigned to correct TSS level · Initial Bitácora profile created · First session mission defined · Coach briefed before entering the water
  • "Diagnosis before prescription. Every time."

TOOL 02 — LEVEL APP

Know your real level.

Before you paddle out.

  • Most surfers think they are better than they are — or worse. The TSS Level App removes the subjectivity.

WHAT THE APP DOES

  • → Evaluates the student across the TSS 6-level progression
  • → Asks structured questions about consistency, conditions, and execution
  • → Outputs an objective level assignment: Beginner → Novice → Foundation → Emerging → Pre-Elite → Elite
  • → Removes coach bias and student ego from the equation
  • → Takes less than 5 minutes to complete

WHY IT MATTERS

Honest starting point

  • Progress is measured from a real baseline — not a hoped-for one.

Correct mission selection

  • The right drills are only useful at the right level. The app ensures alignment.

Student buy-in

  • When students understand their real level, they train with more intention.

Scalable globally

  • Any academy, anywhere, uses the same objective entry standard.

TOOL 04 — STUDENT LEVEL KIT

Complete learning material for every level.

Students don't just train — they study.

  • Each TSS level comes with a complete physical and digital kit. The student leaves every session with material to review, study, and practice.

M1 — Safety & Communication

  • Hand signals, emergency protocols, coach-student communication system, surf etiquette. Non-negotiable foundation.

M2 — Wave Knowledge

  • Parts of a wave, types of waves and breaks, lefts vs rights, wave stages, pocket theory. Ocean literacy.

M3 — Technical Sequence

  • Full progression of drills specific to their level: board control, pop-up, stance, cobra, rotation — step by step.

M4 — Mission Tracking

  • Personal log for each session: time, reps, focus score, difficulty score, next mission. Makes progress visible.

M5 — Mental Framework

  • Introduction to One Wave concepts: focus, training vs free surfing, managing frustration, setting expectations.

M6 — Next Level Preview

  • What the next TSS level looks like — what needs to be mastered to progress. Creates direction and motivation.
  • The kit is the same for every student at the same level — anywhere in the world. Consistency is the point.

TOOL 05 — LEVEL PRESENTATIONS + DRILLS

The coach's pedagogical toolkit.

Every session is a planned mission.

  • For each TSS level, there is a complete set of coach-facing presentations that structure every single session.

WHAT EACH PRESENTATION CONTAINS

🟡 Yellow Slides — In-Water Drills (Coach)

  • Mission for the session · Focus point · What to observe · What to correct · What NOT to correct yet

🟣 Theory Slides — Student Concepts

  • Wave knowledge · Technical principles · Mental concepts aligned with One Wave

🔴 Land Drills

  • Pop-up progressions · Stance work · Rotation mechanics · Skate transfer drills

🎯 Mission Structure

  • One objective per session. Not a list of things to improve — one specific, measurable focus.

📊 Video Analysis Guide

  • What to evaluate · What to ignore · How to give feedback · Suggested duration

DRILL EXAMPLES — NOVICE LEVEL

  • → Control My Board — understand board pivot with hand placement
  • → Find the Sweet Spot — heart-to-board connection before paddling
  • → Turtle Roll: Form the T — align nose with wave direction
  • → Chase the Pocket — identify and move toward the energy source
  • → Cobra + Choose Your Line — invisible surfing before the pop-up
  • → The 2-Second Pop-Up — stability before releasing the board
  • → Crush the Circles — feet connected to the center of the board
  • → Play With the Pocket — integrating game to test the full system
  • "Coaches don't teach movements. They design learning environments." — TSS Academy Licensing Manual

TOOL 06 — BITÁCORA TSS

If it's not recorded,

it didn't happen.

  • The TSS Bitácora is the individual learning record for every student in the system.

STUDENT PROFILE

  • Level · Goals · Background · Learning style · Blockers and fears identified at intake assessment

SESSION LOG

  • Date · Objective · Mission · Key observation · Coach feedback · Next focus area

PROGRESS TRACKING

  • What was trained · Consistency rating · Milestones reached · Level transition readiness

WHY THE BITÁCORA IS A COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE

  • ✓ Students can see their own progress — not feel it, see it. Numbers, dates, missions completed.
  • ✓ Coaches hand off students between sessions without losing continuity. The log is the memory.
  • ✓ Academies can prove ROI to students: 'Here is exactly what you built in 7 days.'
  • ✓ Level transition is based on data, not impression. Progression is objective and verifiable.
  • The Bitácora is part of the Information System — the third pillar that makes TSS scalable and auditable.

TOOL 08 — ACADEMY OPERATIONS MANUAL

The replication standard.

Any academy. Any country.

  • The Operations Manual is what makes TSS licenseable at scale. It is not a guideline — it is a non-negotiable standard.

HARD SYSTEM

(Physical Foundation)

  • · Teaching zones and safe entry/exit
  • · Equipment by level: soft tops → funboards → performance
  • · Coach vs student identification
  • · Beach-ready first aid + emergency plan
  • · Safety signage and TSS visual identity

SOFT SYSTEM

(People + Method)

  • · Instructor pathway: Water Safety → ISA Level 1 → TSS Certification
  • · Level-based teaching: certified coaches teach only certified levels
  • · Teaching language aligned across all coaches
  • · Coaching philosophy: design learning environments, not instruction
  • · Parallel modules: constraint learning, ecology, 7 senses

INFORMATION SYSTEM

(Learning Continuity)

  • · Student profiles maintained from day one
  • · Session documentation: objective, mission, observation, next focus
  • · Bitácora protocol active for all students
  • · Communication flow: before session / after session
  • · Quarterly audit and quality review
  • "The method only works when the three systems are aligned. Equipment alone does not teach. Coaches alone do not scale. Information alone does not produce progress."
