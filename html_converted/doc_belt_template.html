Document Purpose

This document establishes the repeatable master template that every belt section in the TSS Student Progression Manual must follow. Every belt section written from this point forward must conform to this template. No field may be removed. Fields may only be added with formal documentation of the rationale.

The Three Circles of Power — Foundational Role

Before any belt section can be understood, the role of the Three Circles of Power must be clear. They are not a progression track. They are not a belt. They are the shared language that makes progression possible.

E. SYSTEM INTEGRATION

The Three Circles of Power appear in the template in three distinct ways. Understanding these three roles prevents the confusion between foundational language and belt-specific progression.

Role 1: Dedicated Field (Field #07)

Field #07 — Three Circles Engagement — is the primary home. This field captures how the surfer’s relationship with the Three Circles deepens at each belt level. It does not describe a separate progression track. It describes how the same foundational language is understood and applied with increasing sophistication.

Role 2: Diagnostic Lens in Common Mistakes (Field #15)

When documenting common mistakes at each belt, the Three Circles provide the first diagnostic layer. Every error can be traced to a circle: is it a body problem (Circle 1), a board problem (Circle 2), or a wave-reading problem (Circle 3)? The template instructs the writer to tag each common mistake with its primary circle.

Role 3: Implicit Foundation in Technical & Tactical Focus (Fields #06, #08)

The Technical Focus and Tactical Focus fields describe belt-specific sequences and applications. These are built on top of the Three Circles vocabulary. When a Technical Focus section says “rail engagement during bottom turn,” that language only makes sense because Circle 1 (rotation/compression) and Circle 2 (foot placement/board response) have already been established. The Three Circles are the language; the technical and tactical content is the conversation.

Summary: Three Layers of Three Circles Presence

This is the most important distinction in the manual. Confusing these two layers produces either a method that repeats itself at every belt or a method where each belt feels disconnected from the whole.

Foundational Elements (Pre-Belt / Across All Belts)

These elements belong to the method itself, not to any individual belt. They are introduced before the first belt begins and are referenced throughout all seven levels.

Belt-Specific Elements

These elements are unique to each belt and must be rewritten completely for each level.

HOW THE THREE CIRCLES RELATE TO BELTS The Three Circles provide the prerequisite language. The belts provide the progressive application. A White Belt (Beginner) surfer learns the Three Circles as foundational vocabulary. A Purple Belt (Emerging) surfer uses the same vocabulary at higher precision and in more complex conditions. The circles never change. The depth at which each belt engages them does.

PART 1 REFINED MASTER BELT TEMPLATE
#FIELDFILL INSTRUCTIONS
01Belt Name & ColorOfficial name and color. Format: [Color] Belt — [Level Name]. Include hex code for visual materials.
02Developmental Purpose3–5 sentences: what transformation happens here? What does the surfer become by completing this level?
03Sequence FocusWhich sequence numbers this belt covers. What aspects of the full surf sequence are prioritized and why.
04Main Learning ObjectiveOne sentence. The single thing a surfer must achieve to graduate. Must be observable or measurable. If you cannot state it in one sentence, the belt design is not clear.
#FIELDFILL INSTRUCTIONS
05Key Concepts3–7 core concepts (ideas, principles, mental models—not techniques). Format: concept name + one-line explanation.
06Technical FocusSpecific technical elements trained at this belt, organized by the Technical Sequence. Include: which parts of the sequence are being refined, body mechanics, expected quality standard.
07Three Circles EngagementHow each of the Three Circles of Power operates at this belt level. For each circle: what depth of understanding is expected, what new connections the surfer makes, and how the circle is used diagnostically by the coach at this level. This is foundational language, not a progression track—the circles remain the same; the surfer’s engagement with them deepens.
08Tactical FocusHow the surfer applies technique in real conditions. Wave selection, positioning, ocean reading, decision-making expected at this level.
09Mental FocusPsychological and learning skills: emotional regulation, focus strategies, frustration management, self-assessment capacity, flow channel expectations.
10Safety ConsiderationsBelt-specific safety. Not generic—what is uniquely dangerous at THIS level. Ocean condition limits, equipment requirements, supervision ratios, environment restrictions.
#FIELDFILL INSTRUCTIONS
11Learning Blocks InvolvedWhich learning blocks are active at this belt. List each block with its purpose and how it contributes to the belt’s main objective. If the learning block architecture is still evolving, describe blocks by their functional role (what pillar they serve, what environment they use, what outcome they target) rather than referencing a fixed taxonomy.
12Key Drills & MissionsPrimary drills and game-based missions. For each: name, pillar, environment, wave size range, objective, success indicator. Drills are games with intentional constraints (Evolve Through Play).
13Session StructureRecommended session architecture: warm-up, land preparation, water time, drill sequencing, feedback timing, cool-down, session logging. What changes from a standard session at this level.
14Recommended EnvironmentsVenues and conditions appropriate for this belt: venue types, wave size range (min/max), tide/wind conditions, venue-specific adaptations.
#FIELDFILL INSTRUCTIONS
15Common Mistakes5–10 most frequent errors at this level. For each: what it looks like, why it happens, which Circle of Power is typically involved, and correction approach.
16Progression MarkersObservable behaviors indicating progress WITHIN this belt. Intermediate checkpoints between entry and graduation. Use clear language: “the surfer consistently [action] in [condition].” Minimum 5 markers.
17Promotion CriteriaDefinitive checklist for graduation. Technical benchmarks (what the surfer can do), tactical benchmarks (when/where), mental benchmarks (pressure handling), minimum sessions, coach sign-off. Binary: met or not met.
18Red Flags / Non-PromotionIndicators a surfer should NOT be promoted even if some criteria are met. Safety concerns, inconsistency, dependency on coach instruction, mindset gaps that predict failure at the next level.
#FIELDFILL INSTRUCTIONS
19Coach Notes / TrackingWhat the coach should observe and document. Session log priorities, rating scales (Focus 1–5, Frustration 1–5), feedback protocol, video analysis frequency, homework assignments.
20Relationship to the Infinite CircleHow this belt manifests the Infinite Circle. The full sequence (Read → Prepare → Enter → Paddle → Catch → Ride → Exit → Repeat) is present at every level. Which parts receive primary attention? How does depth change from the previous belt?
21Relationship to the Progression MapWhere this belt sits in the overall progression. What comes before it (prerequisite belt and its key outcome). What comes after it (next belt and what it demands). What dependencies this belt has—what must be solid from the previous level for this belt to work. What this belt prepares the surfer for at the next level.
PART 2 WHERE THE THREE CIRCLES BELONG IN THE TEMPLATE
WHAT TO WRITE IN FIELD #07 For each circle (Body, Board, Wave): what level of understanding is expected at this belt? What new connections does the surfer make? How does the coach use this circle diagnostically? The language stays the same. The surfer’s depth with it changes.
EXAMPLE Common Mistake: “Surfer loses balance during bottom turn.” → Primary Circle: Body (Circle 1) — compression timing is off; surfer is extending too early. Secondary Circle: Board (Circle 2) — foot placement not adjusted for the rail change.
WRITING RULE Technical and Tactical Focus fields may reference Three Circles vocabulary but should NOT re-explain the circles. The circles are treated as established language. If a reader does not understand a circle reference, the answer is in the foundational section of the manual, not in the belt chapter.

PART 3 FOUNDATIONAL vs. BELT-SPECIFIC
ELEMENTROLE
Three Circles of PowerThe shared language of body, board, and wave. Introduced as a prerequisite. Every belt references them but never re-teaches them from scratch. Depth increases; the circles themselves do not change.
The Infinite Circle conceptThe philosophical principle that the full sequence repeats forever. Introduced once. Each belt explains how it manifests at that level.
The 4 PillarsPhysical, Technical, Tactical, Mental (+ Safety). The framework for holistic development. Every belt must address all four. The framework is fixed; the content per pillar changes.
Evolve Through Play philosophyThe principle that drills are games with intentional constraints. Established once as method philosophy. Each belt designs its own drills under this principle.
Safety as cross-cuttingSafety is not a pillar—it cuts across everything. The principle is fixed. The specific safety concerns change per belt.
The Progression MapThe visual map of the full journey. Introduced as a structural guide. Each belt shows where it sits, what comes before, and what comes after.
Template structure (21 fields)The repeatable format. Fixed across all belts. This is the system that makes the manual consistent.
Promotion formatBinary checklist format. The structure is always the same. The criteria change per belt.
Session log formatThe data structure for documenting sessions. Same format, different content.

THREE RULES FOR WRITERS Rule 1: Never re-teach the Three Circles inside a belt chapter. Reference them. Use their vocabulary. Show how the surfer’s engagement deepens. But the explanation of what each circle IS belongs in the foundational section of the manual, not in any individual belt. Rule 2: Never describe a belt-specific sequence as if it were the Three Circles. The Three Circles are language. The belt sequence is application. “Bottom turn mechanics” is a belt-specific technical sequence that uses Circle 1 and Circle 2 vocabulary. It is not a circle. Rule 3: The litmus test. If you removed the Three Circles section from the manual entirely, would the belt chapter still make sense as a standalone document? If yes, the circles are properly foundational. If no, you have accidentally made them belt-specific and need to rewrite.
