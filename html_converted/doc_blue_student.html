YOUR JOURNEY

Where You Are

You have completed Yellow Belt. You catch green waves, angle your takeoffs, manage paddling speeds, and ride the wave face with intention. Now it is time to transform those skills from attempted to reliable.

Blue Belt is where surfing becomes real surfing. You will learn to link sections together, generate speed through pumping, execute bottom turns with rail engagement, project toward targets on the wave face, and perform your first named maneuvers.

What Changes at Blue Belt

At Yellow Belt, you attempted skills on green waves. At Blue Belt, those skills must become consistent and repeatable across multiple sessions. The standard shifts from 'I can do it sometimes' to 'I can do it most of the time.'

You will also take full ownership of your sessions. You analyze the venue, select waves tactically, make decisions during the ride, self-correct between waves, and debrief your own performance. The coach confirms and adjusts — but you lead.

The Progression Map

You are here: Blue Belt — Foundation. Sequences #8 through #13.

How to Use This Manual

This manual follows the same structure as your Yellow Belt manual. Each section covers one part of your progression. Scan QR codes for video explanations. Use mission trackers to record your progress. Review before and after every session.

SECTION 1: THE THREE CIRCLES AT BLUE BELT

At Blue Belt, the Three Circles of Power are no longer separate lenses. They operate as an integrated system. You feel how body, board, and wave interact in real time.

Circle 1: Body — Rotation as Line Control

Rotation becomes your primary tool for controlling your line on the wave. Compression and extension are now timed to the wave face shape for pumping and bottom turns. Hold — maintaining a position before releasing — becomes a conscious skill.

Circle 2: Board — Rail Engagement

Rail engagement is the defining board skill at Blue Belt. You experience how weighting one rail creates an arc, how foot placement pressure determines turn radius, and how weight distribution between front and back controls whether you accelerate, hold, or brake.

Circle 3: Wave — Real-Time Reading

You read the wave in real time during the ride. You anticipate where the pocket will be in 2-3 seconds and position accordingly. You understand that the wave face changes shape as it breaks and that your line must adapt.

SECTION 2: NEW TECHNIQUES

Blue Belt introduces the technical elements that transform wave-face riding into connected, expressive surfing.

Pumping for Speed Generation

Pumping is the active version of 'go up and down' from Yellow Belt. Instead of passively riding the wave face, you generate speed through coordinated compression and extension. Compress as you move down the face, extend as you move up. The timing must match the wave's shape.

Medium Bottom Turn

A controlled turn at the bottom of the wave face that engages the rail, generates speed, and creates a line of projection toward the next section. 'Medium' means clear rail engagement and directional change, but not yet the full vertical power turns of higher belts.

The bottom turn is the setup for everything. Without a good bottom turn, no maneuver works. The bottom turn ends when projection begins.

Hold

Hold is the ability to maintain a position, a line, or a rail engagement for a controlled duration before releasing into the next action. Hold is what separates rushed surfing from intentional surfing.

At Yellow Belt, your turns were quick. At Blue Belt, you learn to hold the bottom turn: compress, engage the rail, look at your target, and WAIT before releasing into projection. The hold is where power accumulates.

Projection

Projection is the direction and energy that a bottom turn generates toward the next section of the wave. A good bottom turn projects you toward a target: the lip (for vertical maneuvers), the pocket (for speed recovery), or the shoulder (for a cutback).

Frontside projection: hips up and forward, touch water and throw ball back, keep scapula locked and chest pointing where you want to go, weight on front foot.

Backside projection: look at the target, straighten front leg, choke and point chest where you want to go.

Recovery After Maneuvers

After every turn or maneuver, you must recover: regain stable posture (Circle 1), re-center weight on the board (Circle 2), and re-orient toward the pocket or next section (Circle 3). Recovery is what connects one action to the next. Without recovery, you cannot link.

SECTION 3: NAMED SEQUENCE EXPRESSIONS

Blue Belt introduces the first named actions in the TSS system. These are not tricks or casual nicknames. They are official named sequence expressions that describe specific technical executions built on the PRCH framework.

Frontside Expressions

CRUZ SNAP — A controlled frontside snap executed from projection after a medium bottom turn. Rail engagement, directional change at the top of the wave face, and controlled release. The sequence: bottom turn → hold → projection → Cruz Snap → impulse → recovery.

GRENADE — An explosive energy release. The surfer projects through the turn with maximum commitment. Think of it as a Cruz Snap with more power and less control. The sequence structure is the same, but the intensity at the action point is maximum.

CRUZ CUTBACK — A frontside cutback — turning back toward the pocket when drifting to the shoulder. This is your primary tool for maintaining the pocket relationship through full rides. Sections are linked through cutback flow: ride → drift → cutback → return to pocket → continue.

Backside Expressions

CHOKE — A backside compression concept. Compress through the turn, maintaining control and rail engagement with your body facing away from the wave.

TAPALOCO SNAP — A defined backside snap executed from projection. The equivalent of the Cruz Snap on the backside. The sequence: bottom turn → hold → choke → Tapaloco Snap → elbow engagement → impulse → recovery.

TAPALOCO CUTBACK — A backside cutback linking sections. The equivalent of Cruz Cutback on the backside. Used to return toward the pocket when drifting to the shoulder on backside waves.

ELBOW — Upper-body engagement integrated into the backside turn. The elbow and shoulder drive the rotation when you cannot use visual leading as effectively as on frontside.

SECTION 4: THE SEQUENCE AT BLUE BELT

Your entire sequence from Yellow Belt is still active. Every step still applies. What changes at Blue Belt is the depth of execution and the addition of the blocks that produce real surfing.

BLOCK 1-3 — AUTONOMOUS FOUNDATIONS

Venue Analysis, Goal Setting, Warm-Up, Board Entry, Sweet Spot, Paddle Out, Duck Dive/Turtle Roll, Paddling Speeds, Chase the Pocket, Correct Angle, Cobra — Pick Your Line, Pop-Up, and Foot Position are all expected to be autonomous at Blue Belt. These are your White and Yellow Belt foundations. They should require zero coach direction.

BLOCK 4 — POWER POSTURE (Dynamic Recovery)

Posture at Blue Belt is not just about standing correctly. It is about dynamic recovery. After every turn, every maneuver, every section transition, you must return to power posture. This is the reset that enables the Infinite Circle to continue.

Power posture details: chest points toward the board's nose, get low (90 degrees), knee in front of the toe tip, weight on front foot, active scapula, front hand reaches and crosses, activate hands, arms, and core, keep shoulders on top of or forward of your front foot.

BLOCK 5 — ROTATION + BOTTOM TURN

This is where Blue Belt diverges most from Yellow Belt. Rotation is no longer just for basic turns. It is now the engine of your bottom turn, and the bottom turn is the setup for everything.

Frontside bottom turn: weight on front foot, elbow and forearm close to the water, palm facing down, engage obliques, HOLD.

Backside bottom turn: weight on front foot, front arm at shoulder height, fingertips close to the water, HOLD.

BLOCK 6 — PROJECTION

Projection is the direction and force generated by the bottom turn. It aims you at your target on the wave face. The bottom turn ends when projection begins.

Frontside projection: hips up and forward, touch water and throw ball back, scapula locked and chest pointing where you want to go, weight on front foot.

Backside projection: look at the target, straighten front leg, choke and point chest where you want to go.

BLOCK 7 — MANEUVERS (Rail Transitions)

A maneuver is a change of direction, and direction is changed using the rails. To surf well, you must transition from rail to rail.

Frontside: after projection, perform Cruz (Christ Cross), then Grenade (throw the grenade), and return to posture.

Backside: after the Choke, execute Tapaloco, then Elbow (Codazo), and return to posture.

SECTION 5: THE INFINITE CIRCLE AT BLUE BELT

At Blue Belt, every step of the Infinite Circle is functional and autonomous. The attention shifts from individual steps to the connections between them.

At Yellow Belt, you experienced the circle for the first time. At Blue Belt, you complete multiple loops on a single wave. Each loop connects to the next through recovery. This is wave completion — riding the wave from takeoff to its natural end using all available sections.

SECTION 6: YOUR MISSIONS

Blue Belt missions are organized by sequence progression. Each mission builds on the previous one. Focus on one mission per session.

MISSION 1 Play with the Pocket

▸ Go into the water and play with the pocket: stay close, move away, return

▸ Avoid getting caught in the whitewater

▸ When a wave is about to close, play with the pocket ahead of the closing section

▸ Focus on connecting feet to the board — good foot positioning throughout

MISSION 2 Connect with Board + Foot Positions

▸ Go into the water, connect with the board

▸ Move foot to position 1 and return to the whitewater

▸ Add impulse whenever you need speed (momentum)

▸ Focus on deliberate foot position switches between position 1 and 2

MISSION 3 Pumping + Return to Pocket

▸ Perform a pump and return to the pocket

▸ If the wave is fast, continue pumping to maintain speed

▸ Practice frontside and backside pumping

▸ Focus on compression/extension timing with the wave face

MISSION 4 Bottom Turn + Hold (Frontside)

▸ Execute a frontside bottom turn with rail engagement

▸ HOLD the compression at the bottom before releasing

▸ Focus on vision: look where you want to project

▸ Return to posture after the bottom turn

MISSION 5 Bottom Turn + Hold (Backside)

▸ Execute a backside bottom turn with rail engagement

▸ HOLD — trust the rotation with your back to the wave

▸ Focus on head turn leading the rotation

▸ Return to posture after the bottom turn

MISSION 6 Bottom Turn + Projection (Frontside)

▸ Bottom turn with hold, then project toward the lip or pocket

▸ Practice projection: hips up, scapula locked, chest pointing at target

▸ Feel the energy transfer from bottom turn to projection direction

▸ Recover after projection: impulse + posture

MISSION 7 Bottom Turn + Projection (Backside)

▸ Backside bottom turn with choke

▸ Project: look at target, straighten front leg, point chest

▸ Recovery after projection

▸ Repeat across multiple waves

MISSION 8 Cruz Snap (Frontside Maneuver)

▸ Bottom turn → hold → projection → Cruz Snap

▸ Focus on the full sequence, not just the snap

▸ Cristo Cross: chest aligned with or ahead of hips, look over shoulder, engage obliques

▸ Recovery: impulse + return to posture

MISSION 9 Tapaloco Snap (Backside Maneuver)

▸ Bottom turn → hold → choke → Tapaloco Snap

▸ Elbow engagement to drive the rotation

▸ Touch the board, add impulse

▸ Recovery: return to posture

MISSION 10 Rail Transitions — Frontside Flow

▸ Projection → Cruz → Grenade → return to posture

▸ Focus on rail-to-rail transition fluidity

▸ Each movement flows into the next — no stops between actions

MISSION 11 Rail Transitions — Backside Flow

▸ Choke → Tapaloco → Elbow → return to posture

▸ Focus on backside fluidity

▸ Trust the rotation even without full visual leading

MISSION 12 Cruz Cutback — Return to Pocket (Frontside)

▸ When drifting to the shoulder, execute Cruz Cutback

▸ Turn back toward the pocket

▸ Re-engage with the energy source

▸ Continue the ride after the cutback — linking sections

MISSION 13 Tapaloco Cutback — Return to Pocket (Backside)

▸ Backside cutback linking sections

▸ Turn back toward the pocket on backside waves

▸ Hold → Elbow → impulse → posture → continue riding

MISSION 14 Complete a Circle (Wave Completion)

▸ Complete one full Infinite Circle loop on a single wave

▸ Posture → bottom turn → hold → projection → maneuver → recovery → posture

▸ If the wave allows, attempt a second loop

▸ The mission is the full architecture, not any single move

SECTION 7: ON-LAND DRILLS

Blue Belt drills shift from single-skill practice to connected-sequence practice. You are training the full chain, not isolated movements.

Drill L1: Stance + Rotation on Flat Ground

Identify your comfortable stance. Shoulders and hips facing forward, weight on front foot, back knee pointing forward, active scapula, elbows close to ribs. Then practice rotation: look where you want to go, engage obliques, keep weight on front foot, foot centered on board.

Drill L2: Pop-Up + Foot Position Switches

Pop-up and hold for 2 seconds. Switch foot from position 2 to position 1 and back, depending on the line you want to draw. Repeat 5 times.

Drill L3: Rotation on Skateboard

Set up cones and chalk lines. Practice frontside rotation 4-8 times, then backside 4-8 times. Then add a turn followed by impulse + back to posture and continue straight. Chest should be aligned over front foot or slightly forward.

Drill L4: Pumping on Skateboard

Frontside pump: posture → projection + extend front leg → swimming arm motion (elbow higher than head) → get low → back to posture. Backside pump: posture → extend legs → arms straight toward nose direction → switch rails with M-motion → get low → back to posture. Practice 10-20 repetitions without skateboard first, then on the skateboard.

Drill L5: Bottom Turn + Projection (Flat Ground)

Start in posture. Perform simple rotation with deeper flexion and hold the position. Add projection by extending legs and using the arm. Draw a U-shape on the ground and simulate following the line with rotation and projection at the marked spot. Repeat 5 times.

Drill L6: Bottom Turn + Projection on Skateboard

Same exercise on skateboard following chalk line. Execute: Posture + Rotation + Hold + Projection. Finish the maneuver upon completing the rotation. Repeat 4-8 times.

Drill L7: Rail Transition Drill (Full Sequence)

Practice the complete sequence on flat ground, then with chalk line, then on skateboard. The sequence: Bottom Turn + Hold → Projection → Cruz + Granada (frontside) or Tapaloco + Codazo (backside) → Return to posture. Repeat 4-8 times.

Drill L8: Guided Visualization

Visualize yourself doing the maneuver. Focus on the goal you have chosen. Guided group breathing session. 5 minutes of visualization practice. See the wave, see the pocket, see yourself completing the circle.

SECTION 8: KEY CONCEPTS & SELF-IMPROVEMENT

Quick Reference: Blue Belt Concepts

Self-Improvement Tool: 3 Questions

Use these three questions after every session to discover what to improve:

1. What do I want to do but can't? (Example: I want to turn better but I lose speed.)

2. Is it an execution or decision-making problem? Execution = I'm not doing it correctly. Decision = I'm not choosing the right moment or place.

3. What is causing it? Physical (strength, balance, endurance), Mental (hesitation, fear, slow reactions), Technical (mechanical details, timing), or Tactical (wave usage, reading, positioning).

SECTION 9: YOUR PROGRESSION

Early Blue Belt (Sequences #8-#9)

You complete recognizable frontside rides on most catchable waves. You attempt backside with intention. Pumping is functional. Takeoff angle is automatic on both sides. Session independence is full.

Mid Blue Belt (Sequences #10-#11)

You execute medium bottom turns with visible rail engagement on frontside. Backside shows intentional rail engagement. You project after the bottom turn toward a recognizable target. First maneuver attempts show connection to the bottom turn. You recover after maneuvers.

Late Blue Belt (Sequences #12-#13)

You link two or more named actions on a single wave. Cutback is active on both sides when drifting to the shoulder. Wave completion is the norm. Self-correction is visible between waves. You can articulate your line plan before paddling.

What Comes Next: Purple Belt (Emerging)

At Purple Belt, you move from consistency to expression. Maneuver variety increases. Snaps, floaters, vertical turns. Personal style emerges. You begin to develop a surfing identity. The fundamentals from Blue Belt are the foundation that makes expression possible.
