SECTION 1: CERTIFICATION PHILOSOPHY

The TSS Coach Formation Principle

A coach first LIVES the method as a student, then LEARNS to teach it.

Formation Sequence

Experience it

Understand it

Learn to teach it

Deliver it with consistency

Certification Characteristics

Portable: Travel with the coach

Auditable: TSS Global can verify

Renewable: Annually

Final certification authority: TSS Global (Enkrateia SA de CV). No individual coach can grant TSS certification independently.

SECTION 2: CERTIFICATION LEVELS OVERVIEW

SECTION 3: LEVEL 1 — FOUNDATION COACH

3.1 Prerequisites

Minimum Blue Belt

Demonstrated understanding of White and Yellow Belt sequences

Clean safety record

Minimum age: 18

3.2 Scope

Teaches White Belt (Seq #1-#5) and Yellow Belt (Seq #6-#7)

Can work in water up to 3 feet

Whitewater and small green waves

3.3 Core Competencies

Safety management for beginners (board handling, zone awareness, ratios)

Session structure using 3 Moments (Context, Planning, Close)

Drill delivery using EDPF method (Explain, Demonstrate, Participate, Feedback)

Board transition assessment (5-point checklist for hardtop clearance)

Junior adaptations (playful drills, shorter sessions, mandatory groups for <12)

P·R·C·H teaching at fundamental level

Three Circles of Power — Surfer Version (Body, Feet Position, Wave)

Block 0-3 delivery (Orientation, Preparation, Wave Entry, Pop-Up)

One correction per wave discipline

Surfer-speaks-first feedback protocol

3.4 Assessment

Knowledge Check: Written exam on TSS methodology (White/Yellow belt content, safety protocols, session structure)

Practical Demonstration: Execute all White and Yellow belt sequences at minimum Competent level

Teaching Practicum: Deliver 3 supervised sessions to White/Yellow belt students. Observed by L3+ coach.

Portfolio: Session logs from practicum, self-evaluation, observer feedback.

3.5 Restrictions

Cannot teach Blue Belt or above

Cannot modify session structure

Cannot assess for belt promotion without L2+ supervisor present

SECTION 4: LEVEL 2 — PRACTITIONER COACH

4.1 Prerequisites

Minimum Purple Belt

Active L1 certification for minimum 6 months

Minimum 50 logged sessions as L1

Clean safety record

4.2 Scope

Teaches White through Blue Belt (Seq #1-#13)

Can work in conditions up to 6 feet

4.3 Core Competencies (all L1 + these)

Blue Belt sequence delivery (#8-#13)

Named expression teaching (Cruz Snap, Tapaloco Snap, Cutback, etc.)

TSS Execution Sequence Chains — Frontside and Backside

Infinite Circle coaching and diagnosis

Block System diagnostic: identify which block breaks and correct one level back

Progressive rating scales (Composure replaces Frustration at Blue)

Session design for intermediate surfers

Competition path vs. Recreational path awareness

Coach feedback documentation (digital via TSS Brain)

Student continuity management (returning students, history review)

4.4 Assessment

Knowledge Check: Written + oral exam on full methodology through Blue Belt

Practical Demonstration: Execute all sequences through Blue Belt at minimum 7/10

Teaching Practicum: Deliver 5 supervised sessions across White-Blue

Diagnostic Assessment: Given video of a Blue Belt surfer, identify failing block and propose correction

Portfolio: 50+ session logs, student progression evidence, self-reflection

4.5 Restrictions

Cannot teach Purple+ content

Cannot certify other coaches

SECTION 5: LEVEL 3 — ADVANCED COACH

5.1 Prerequisites

Minimum Brown Belt

Active L2 for minimum 12 months

Minimum 150 logged sessions

Demonstrated mentoring of at least 2 L1 coaches

5.2 Scope

Teaches all levels through Brown Belt (Seq #1-#22)

Mentors L1 and L2 coaches

Can observe and evaluate teaching practicums

5.3 Core Competencies (all L2 + these)

Purple and Brown Belt sequence delivery

Aerial System teaching (Ramp → Air → Landing)

Advanced combo coaching

Condition adaptation training

Competition preparation (ISA/WSL scoring criteria, heat strategy, pre-competition routines)

Board selection knowledge by conditions

Coach mentoring skills

Assessment design for teaching practicums

TSS Brain: full data interpretation for student progression

Conflict resolution in academy settings

5.4 Assessment

Knowledge Check: Comprehensive exam covering full methodology including Brown Belt

Practical Demonstration: Execute sequences through Brown Belt at 8/10+

Mentoring Portfolio: Evidence of mentoring 2+ L1 coaches with documented progression

Teaching Practicum: Deliver 3 sessions to Purple/Brown students

Competition Coaching Demo: Design and execute a competition preparation session

SECTION 6: LEVEL 4 — MASTER COACH

6.1 Prerequisites

Minimum Black Belt

Active L3 for minimum 24 months

Minimum 300 logged sessions

ISA/WSL judge certification (competitive path)

6.2 Scope

Teaches ALL levels including Black Belt (Seq #1-#26)

Mentors L1-L3 coaches

6.3 Core Competencies (all L3 + these)

Black Belt content delivery (total mastery, knowledge transmission, maximum potential)

Full pillar integration coaching (Physical, Technical, Tactical, Mental at deepest level)

Performance science integration (biology, psychology, recovery protocols)

Elite competition coaching (score management 8+, heat strategy advanced)

Academy quality assurance

Full system architecture understanding

Curriculum development assistance

6.4 Assessment

Knowledge Check: Complete system mastery exam covering all 26 sequences

Practical Demonstration: Black Belt level execution

System Architecture Presentation: Present complete TSS methodology to a panel

Teaching Practicum: Teach across all belt levels

Portfolio: 300+ session logs, mentoring evidence, competition results if applicable

SECTION 7: LEVEL 5 — COACH EDUCATOR

7.1 Prerequisites

Black Belt

Active L4 for minimum 36 months

Minimum 500 logged sessions

Demonstrated development of at least 5 coaches from L1 to L2+

7.2 Scope

Does NOT teach surfers directly

Forms, evaluates, and certifies coaches

Designs curriculum

Ensures quality across academies

7.3 Core Competencies (all L4 + these)

Certification assessment design and delivery

Coach evaluation rubric application

Curriculum design and updating

Academy audit and compliance verification

TSS Global standards enforcement

Training program design for new coach candidates

Quality control across multiple academies

Cross-cultural coaching adaptation

7.4 Assessment

Panel Review: Presentation to TSS Global board on methodology understanding

Assessment Design: Create a complete L1 certification assessment package

Coach Development Evidence: Documented development of 5+ coaches

System Contribution: Evidence of contribution to TSS methodology development

SECTION 8: ANNUAL RENEWAL

All certifications require annual renewal. Requirements:

Active teaching: Minimum sessions logged per year (L1: 30, L2: 40, L3: 50, L4: 40, L5: N/A)

Continuing education: Attend TSS annual update workshop or equivalent

Safety compliance: No unresolved safety incidents

TSS Brain compliance: Session logging up to date

Renewal fee paid to TSS Global

Failure to renew: Certification becomes "Inactive." Coach cannot teach under TSS brand until renewed. Reactivation requires abbreviated assessment.

SECTION 9: ETHICAL STANDARDS

Coaches represent TSS methodology, not personal opinions

No modification of sequences, drills, or progression without authorization

Safety is always the primary concern — no session continues if safety is compromised

Honest assessment — no inflated belt promotions

Confidentiality of student information

No certification granted outside official TSS channels

Respect for the student's pace — progression is earned, not rushed

SECTION 10: CERTIFICATION PATHWAY DIAGRAM

Surfer Journey

White → Yellow → Blue → Purple → Brown → Black

Coach Entry Points

After Blue Belt → can enter L1 pathway

After Purple Belt → can enter L2 pathway

After Brown Belt → can enter L3 pathway

After Black Belt → can enter L4 pathway

After L4 experience → can enter L5 pathway

CONSTITUTIONAL STATEMENT

The authority to certify TSS coaches belongs exclusively to TSS Global (Enkrateia SA de CV).

No academy, no individual coach, and no partner organization can independently grant, modify, or extend TSS certification.

The certification is the system's quality control mechanism. Without it, the method cannot be trusted to scale.
