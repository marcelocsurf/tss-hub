Purpose of This Document

This framework maps the three operational systems required to implement The Surf Sequence® at Puro Surf Academy — and eventually at any licensed academy. It inventories what exists, identifies what is missing, defines dependencies, and establishes the build order.

How to Read This Document

Each system section contains: a definition, a complete inventory table with status codes, identified gaps, dependencies on the other two systems, and a prioritized action list.

Status codes: GREEN = complete and operational. AMBER = exists but needs formalization or update. RED = does not exist yet and must be built.

Owner codes: MC = Marcelo Castellanos. PS = Puro Surf operations. TSS = TSS Global (Enkrateia). DEV = Development (Brain App).

HARD SYSTEM

Physical infrastructure, equipment, materials, and tangible assets

Definition

The Hard System is everything that exists in the physical world. What you can touch, see, count, and audit. It includes surfboards, wetsuits, facilities, printed materials, safety equipment, signage, and transport. A licensing auditor could verify every item on a walk-through.

SOFT SYSTEM

Processes, protocols, culture, and operational flows

Definition

The Soft System is how things are done. It converts doctrine into daily behavior. It includes every protocol from the moment a student books to the moment they leave, every coach workflow, every safety procedure, and the culture that makes the method feel consistent regardless of which coach is delivering.

INFO SYSTEM

Digital platform, tools, data capture, and operational visibility

Definition

The Info System is TSS Brain and its ecosystem of digital tools. It captures what happens in the Hard and Soft systems, makes it searchable, trackable, and auditable. Without it, the method depends on individual memory. With it, the method scales to any number of academies.

SYSTEM DEPENDENCIES & BUILD ORDER

Cross-System Dependencies

The three systems are not independent. Each has requirements on the others.

  • Hard → Soft: Equipment standards (board sizes, flag colors) must exist before coach protocols can reference them. The ratio matrix needs physical flags to implement formations.
  • Soft → Hard: Operational protocols determine what equipment is needed. The Academy Operations Manual defines the physical requirements list.
  • Soft → Info: The session flow protocol defines what TSS Brain must capture. Build the process first, then digitize it.
  • Info → Soft: Once Brain captures session data, coach evaluation and student progression become data-driven instead of opinion-based.
  • Info → Hard: Brain can track equipment condition, replacement cycles, and audit compliance — but only after P3+ is built.

Strategic Context TSS operates through three interdependent systems. The Hard System is the physical infrastructure. The Soft System is the operational processes and protocols. The Info System is the digital platform that captures, connects, and scales. None works without the others. The Hard System without the Soft System is equipment without process. The Soft System without the Info System is process that depends on individual memory. The Info System without the other two is a platform with nothing to track.
