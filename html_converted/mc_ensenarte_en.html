What is EnseñARTE?

Concept

EnseñARTE is a methodology born from original artworks created to convey the principles of surfing in a visual, sensory, and memorable way. Each painting encodes technical, tactical, and mental concepts from the TSS method.

Role in TSS

Complementary teaching tool for the TSS belt system.

Bridge between the abstract (concepts) and the concrete (execution in the water).

Visual reinforcement resource for coaches and students.

Aligned with the 4 Pillars + Safety Layer of the TSS Canon.

Anxiety

External Force vs. Internal Force

External Force

Comes from the environment:

Wave pitch / steepness

Wind

Water speed

Surface texture

Internal Force

Generated by your body:

Extension and flexion

Torque (rotation)

Compression

Energy release

Surfing is the dialogue between these two forces. The goal: to draw on the face of the wave the lines you want to draw.

Mental Toughness — Danaher System Applied to Surf

Get familiar with chaos

Expose yourself to controlled pressure until your nervous system learns there is no danger.

Train as if competing
Remove the extras. Simulate the real environment with full intention.
Defense before offense
Learn to breathe in bad positions. Build technique from calm.
Redefine your relationship with failure
Failing = being at the right level of challenge. Errors are feedback.
Turn the system into habits
Persistence > inspiration. Decisions based on structure, not emotions.

Mental Pyramid: 1. Not to lose → 2. To cruise → 3. To improve → 4. To compete → 5. To evolve

Sequence Blocks — Session Structure

Block 0: Pre-Surf
  • Venue analysis, set goals, safety, warm up, simulation, sweet spot, paddling, duck dive
Block 1: Catching the Wave
  • Paddling angle, chest pressure, cobra + angle, choose direction
Block 2: Pop Up
  • Ninja / Regular / Pro pop up, feet position, board connection
Block 3: Power Posture
  • Chest to board nose, get low, knee in, active scapula, front hand reach
Block 5: Rotation
  • Oblique + gaze. Frontside: weight on front foot, look direction. Backside: engage oblique
Block 6: Projection
  • More angle and speed. Frontside: hips up, touch water. Backside: extend leg, choke
Block 7: Maneuvers
  • Look where you want to go. Frontside: shoulder + oblique. Backside: elbow + posture
  • Each block distinguishes Frontside vs. Backside instructions — aligned with the TSS Canon Sequence Architecture.

Mindset: Impostor + Identity

Impostor Syndrome

Feeling like you don't deserve to be where you are. That at any moment they'll discover you don't know that much. This feeling is universal and intensifies when you're growing. It's a sign you're in the right place.

You don't have to stay loyal to your character

You can change. The identity you built until today doesn't define what you can be tomorrow. Surfing teaches this every day: every wave is new, every session is a reset. Evolution > loyalty to the past.

TSS Connection: Belt progression normalizes growth. Each new level generates discomfort — that's by design, not weakness. The system is built so you advance by accepting you're a beginner again at each stage.

3 Circles of Learning

Technique

  • The how of movement. The correct mechanical sequence for each TSS block. This is where the diagnostic system lives: identify the first breaking point and apply the right drill.

Ocean + Conditions

  • The environment. Reading the wave, wind, current, bottom. The surfer doesn't control the environment — they interpret it. Ecological Dynamics: use environmental constraints as teacher.

Mental: Learning Process

  • How you learn to learn. Focus, presence, error management, impostor syndrome, flow state. Without the mental pillar, technique doesn't consolidate.
Real learning happens at the intersection of all three circles.
