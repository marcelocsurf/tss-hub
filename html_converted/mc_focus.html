WHAT FOCUS REALLY IS

Focus is not just “paying attention.” Focus is the ability to direct your attention to the right thing, at the right moment, for the right duration — and to shift it when the situation changes. In surfing, the “right thing” changes every second. You scan the horizon, then narrow to one wave, then feel your body, then look at your target, then read the section ahead. The demands shift constantly. The best surfers don’t just focus harder. They focus differently at each moment.

FOCUS SHIFTS IN A SINGLE WAVE

Sitting in lineup

Broad External

  • Scan horizon, read sets, observe conditions, position relative to peak

Set approaches

Broad External → Internal

  • Evaluate: is this my wave? Strategy: paddle or wait?

Decision to paddle

Narrow Internal

  • Commit. Feel body position. Run takeoff sequence mentally.

Paddling for wave

Narrow External

  • Lock onto wave angle. Feel the lift. See the moment to pop up.

Pop-up + drop

Narrow External

  • Execute. Eyes on line. Feet land. Target the pocket.

Bottom turn

Narrow Ext + Internal

  • Look at target (external) + feel compression and rail (internal).

Maneuver

Narrow External

  • Lock onto lip/pocket. Rail transition. Spray. Recovery.

Recovery / next section

Broad External

  • Re-scan. Where’s the pocket? What’s the wave doing next?

AQUÍ Y AHORA.

HERE AND NOW.

The #1 focus error in surfing: drifting to the past or the future.

Dante Nieri (Psicología del Surf) documented the most common mental traps in competitive surfers. They are the same traps that affect every level of student.

The surfer must focus on the HERE and the NOW:

the surfing, the strategy, the wave, the execution.

6 / 15

THE TWO TRAPS

TRAPPED IN THE PAST
“I always lose.”
“This beach never works for me.”
“That rival always beats me.”
“The same thing always happens.” “The waves never come to me.”
TRAPPED IN THE FUTURE
“If I win this, I’ll move up.” “What will my friends think?” “I’m going to celebrate after.” “Next round I’ll face that surfer.” “What if I fall on this wave?”
The only productive time is NOW. The only productive place is HERE. The wave, the lineup, the body, the execution. Everything else is noise.

ATTENTIONAL FLEXIBILITY

The key skill is not focus intensity. It is focus flexibility. Nideffer & Sagal (2006): Athletes who can seamlessly shift between the four quadrants of attention outperform those who are stuck in one mode. In surfing, this is especially critical because the attentional demands change every few seconds. A surfer who is stuck in broad-external mode (scanning) will hesitate to commit. A surfer stuck in narrow-internal (overthinking) will miss the wave entirely. Flexibility is trainable. Like physical flexibility, it improves with deliberate practice.

WHAT HAPPENS WHEN FOCUS FAILS

The Choking Process — Nideffer & Sagal (2006)

1
Pressure increases
Competition heat, big wave, audience, consequences.
2
Muscle tension rises
Body tightens. Breathing shortens. Heart rate spikes.
3
Anxiety narrows focus
Tunnel vision. Can’t see the full wave. Miss cues.
4
Attention shifts inward
Instead of reading the wave, the surfer is thinking about failing.
5
Execution degrades
Late commitment, rushed pop-up, missed pocket, poor timing.
6
Negative spiral
Bad ride → more self-criticism → more tension → worse next wave.

The antidote: trained focus flexibility + breathing + re-centering protocol.

5 FOCUS EXERCISES FOR SURFERS

01

Candle Focus (Narrow-Ext)
  • Stare at a candle flame for 2 minutes without blinking. When your mind wanders, notice it and return. Trains sustained narrow-external attention.

02

Peripheral Vision Walk (Broad-Ext)
  • Walk on the beach. Without moving your eyes, expand awareness to the edges of your visual field. Notice movement at the periphery. Trains broad scanning.

03

Quad Switching Drill
  • Sit in the lineup. Cycle through all 4 quadrants: scan the ocean (broad-ext), plan your next wave (broad-int), feel your body (narrow-int), lock onto one point on the horizon (narrow-ext). 15 sec each. Repeat.

04

Thought Labeling (Present Anchor)
  • During a session, notice when your mind drifts to past or future. Label it silently: ‘past’ or ‘future.’ Then return to: ‘What’s happening NOW?’ Builds here-and-now discipline.

05

One-Word Mission (Pre-Wave)
  • Before each wave, choose ONE word: ‘commit,’ ‘hold,’ ‘project,’ ‘low.’ That word becomes your entire focus for that ride. One word = maximum clarity.

RE-CENTERING PROTOCOL

When focus breaks mid-session — use this in under 30 seconds

STOP

Recognize: “My mind has left the present.” Name it: “past” or “future.”

BREATHE

One 4-7-8 cycle. Inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8. Parasympathetic reset.

FEEL

Notice 3 things: water temperature, board under feet, wind on face. Anchor to NOW.

CHOOSE

Pick ONE word for the next wave. That is your entire focus. Nothing else.

GO

Paddle. Commit. Execute. Review AFTER the ride, not during.

STOP → BREATHE → FEEL → CHOOSE → GO

FOCUS MAP — BY SESSION PHASE

Before session

Broad Internal

  • Set intention. Review mission. Choose 1 focus word for today.

Warm-up / land drill

Narrow Internal

  • Feel body. Visualize movement. Rehearse sequence mentally.

Paddle out

Broad External

  • Read conditions. Observe other surfers. Find the peak. Position.

Waiting for waves

Broad Ext → Int

  • Scan horizon + plan. Which wave? What strategy? Stay calm.

Committing to wave

Narrow External

  • Lock on. Paddle. No hesitation. Eyes on target. Execute.

Riding the wave

Narrow Ext + switching

  • Read section → execute → recover → read next. Rapid shifts.

Between waves

Broad Internal

  • Debrief last wave silently. Adjust. Reset. Breathe.

Post-session

Broad Internal

  • 3 Questions (TSS): What did I want? Was it execution or decision? What caused it?

IN TSS, FOCUS IS NOT AN EXTRA.

IT IS THE OPERATING SYSTEM.

Pilar MEN (Mental): Presence, Motivation, Relaxation, Visualization. Presence IS focus. It is the first component of the Mental Pillar because without it, nothing else works.
The Block System: Each Block requires a different focus type. Block 1 (Preparation) = Broad Internal. Block 2 (Entry) = Broad External. Block 3 (Wave) = Narrow External shifting. Block 4–7 = Rapid switching between all four quadrants.
The Infinite Circle: Posture → Bottom Turn → Hold → Projection → Maneuver → Recovery. Each step has a different attentional demand. The circle only completes when focus is continuous and flexible.
The 3 Questions: Post-session self-assessment IS a Broad Internal focus exercise. What did I want? Execution or decision? What caused it? This trains analytical attention.
