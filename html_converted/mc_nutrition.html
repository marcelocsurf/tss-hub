WHY NUTRITION

MATTERS

This is not a diet. This is a fuel strategy.

BRAIN

  • Decision speed, focus, and reaction time depend on blood glucose & hydration

BODY

  • Muscle endurance, recovery speed, and injury prevention are nutrition-dependent

STAMINA

  • A 2-hour surf session can burn 400–700 kcal. Without fuel strategy, you fade.
  • "A surfer who doesn't manage nutrition is leaving 20–30% of their performance in the kitchen."

THE 3 MACROS

Your body runs on three fuel types. Know them.

CARBS

  • FUEL
  • Primary energy source. Glycogen stores power paddling & explosive pop-ups. Complex carbs = sustained release.
  • Rice, oats, sweet potato, banana, quinoa, pasta

PROTEIN

  • REPAIR
  • Rebuilds muscle fibers damaged during sessions. Essential for adaptation & strength gains. 1.6g/kg/day = optimal plateau (Morton et al., 2018).
  • Chicken, fish, eggs, beans, Greek yogurt, tofu

FATS

  • ENDURANCE + BRAIN
  • Slow-burn energy for long sessions. Essential for brain function, hormone production, and joint health. Omega-3s reduce inflammation.
  • Avocado, nuts, olive oil, salmon, seeds, coconut

PROTEIN

  • THE REPAIR SYSTEM

1.6

g/kg/day

  • Optimal daily protein intake
  • for muscle adaptation
  • (Morton et al., 2018)
  • TOP SOURCES FOR SURFERS
  • Chicken breast — 31g per 100g
  • Salmon — 25g per 100g + Omega-3
  • Eggs — 6g each, complete amino profile
  • Greek yogurt — 10g per 100g + probiotics
  • Lentils/beans — 9g per 100g + fiber
  • Whey protein — 20–25g per scoop (post-session)
  • FOR A 70KG SURFER
  • 70 kg × 1.6 g = 112g protein/day — spread across 4 meals = ~28g per meal.
  • This is about a chicken breast + a handful of nuts per meal, or a protein shake + eggs at breakfast.

HEALTHY FATS

  • ENDURANCE + BRAIN FUEL
OMEGA-3
  • Anti-inflammatory powerhouse. Reduces joint pain, speeds recovery, improves brain function.
  • Salmon, sardines, walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseed
MONOUNSATURATED
  • Heart health, sustained energy, supports hormone production (testosterone, cortisol regulation).
  • Avocado, olive oil, almonds, peanut butter
MCTs
  • Medium-chain triglycerides. Rapid energy without insulin spike. Brain fuel.
  • Coconut oil, coconut milk

NUTRIENT

TIMING

WHEN you eat is almost as important as WHAT you eat.

  • ISSN POSITION STAND
  • Kerksick et al., 2017
  • Post-exercise protein + carbs within
  • 30–60 min maximizes glycogen
  • resynthesis and muscle protein synthesis.
  • Recommended ratio:
4:1 CARBS : PROTEIN
  • META-ANALYSIS 2025
  • Front. Nutr., 2025
  • Immediate post-exercise protein
  • supplementation produces significantly
  • greater muscle recovery and strength
  • gains compared to delayed intake.
  • The window matters.
  • PRACTICAL RULE: Within 30 minutes post-session, consume protein + fast carbs + hydration.
  • Example: Protein shake + banana + 500ml water with electrolytes.

ANTI-INFLAMMATORY

FOODS

Recovery accelerators — your body's repair kit.

OMEGA-3 FATTY ACIDS

  • Salmon, mackerel, sardines, walnuts, chia
  • Reduce prostaglandin-mediated inflammation

TURMERIC (CURCUMIN)

  • Fresh turmeric, golden milk, supplements
  • Blocks NF-κB inflammatory pathway

GINGER

  • Fresh ginger, ginger tea, ginger shots
  • Inhibits COX-2 enzyme (like natural ibuprofen)

BERRIES

  • Blueberries, açaí, strawberries, dark cherries
  • Anthocyanins reduce oxidative stress

LEAFY GREENS

  • Spinach, kale, swiss chard, arugula
  • Vitamin K + folate support tissue repair

PRO-INFLAMMATORY

FOODS

Recovery killers — avoid or minimize.

ALCOHOL

-70%

  • HGH SUPPRESSION
  • Alcohol suppresses human growth hormone by up to 70% overnight. This is your primary muscle repair hormone. One heavy night = days of impaired recovery.
EXCESS SUGAR

↑

  • INSULIN SPIKES
  • Chronic high sugar intake promotes systemic inflammation, impairs immune function, and destabilizes energy levels during sessions.
FRIED / PROCESSED FOOD

↑↑

  • OXIDATIVE STRESS
  • Trans fats and seed oils increase inflammatory markers. Heavy meals pre-session divert blood to digestion, reducing muscle performance.

PRE-SESSION MEAL

  • 2 HOURS BEFORE — FUEL LOADING
  • GOAL: Load glycogen stores without feeling heavy. Complex carbs + moderate protein + low fat.

OPTION A

  • Oatmeal with banana, honey,
  • and a handful of almonds

OPTION B

  • Brown rice + grilled chicken breast
  • + steamed vegetables

OPTION C

  • Whole grain toast + scrambled eggs
  • + avocado slice
  • AVOID PRE-SESSION
  • Heavy fried food • High-fat meals • Large portions < 1hr before • Excess fiber • Spicy food
  • These divert blood to digestion → less blood for muscles → reduced performance + potential nausea.

POST-SESSION MEAL

  • WITHIN 30 MINUTES — THE GOLDEN WINDOW
  • GOAL: Fast glycogen replenishment + protein synthesis activation. Ratio 4:1 carbs:protein.

OPTION A

  • Protein shake (whey 25g) +
  • banana + 500ml electrolyte water

OPTION B

  • White rice + grilled fish +
  • steamed greens + coconut water

OPTION C

  • Greek yogurt + granola +
  • honey + mixed berries
  • THE SCIENCE
  • Post-exercise, GLUT4 transporters are maximally active for ~30–60 minutes — glucose uptake into muscle cells is
  • 2–3x faster than at rest. Missing this window doesn't ruin recovery, but delays it significantly.

DO vs DON'T

DO

  • Eat complex carbs 2hrs before session
  • Hydrate with electrolytes pre & post
  • Protein + carbs within 30 min after
  • Include anti-inflammatory foods daily
  • Check urine color every morning
  • Eat balanced plate (50/30/20)
  • Pack portable snacks between sessions
  • Sleep-promoting foods at dinner

DON'T

  • Alcohol night before session days
  • Caffeine after 2:00 PM
  • Skip meals → energy crash in water
  • Sugary drinks as hydration
  • Surf on an empty stomach
  • Ignore thirst signals
  • Rely on willpower instead of fuel

WHAT TO AVOID

Evidence-based performance killers.

ALCOHOL (post-session)

  • -70% HGH overnight
  • Suppresses growth hormone release during sleep — primary recovery window destroyed. Dehydrates. Impairs protein synthesis by 24%.

CAFFEINE AFTER 2 PM

  • Sleep latency +40 min
  • Caffeine half-life is 5–6 hours. Afternoon coffee disrupts deep sleep (Walker, 2017). Deep sleep = when muscles repair.

HEAVY MEALS PRE-SESSION

  • Blood flow diverted to gut
  • Digestion demands 20–30% of cardiac output. Less blood for working muscles → early fatigue, nausea, cramping risk.

DEHYDRATION (IGNORING THIRST)

  • 2% loss = 20–25% decline
  • Wittbrodt & Millard-Stafford (2018): Even mild dehydration impairs attention, motor coordination, and decision-making.
