PILLAR 1 — SLEEP

Sleep is not rest. Sleep is active repair and learning consolidation.

Growth hormone
  • 80% of HGH released during deep sleep. No deep sleep = no muscle repair.
Motor learning
  • Brain replays and refines motor patterns from the day. Your bottom turn improves while you sleep.
Reaction time
  • One poor night = 300% slower reactions. Equivalent to being legally drunk (Walker, 2017).
Immune function
  • Sleep deprivation reduces immune function by up to 70% (Besedovsky et al., 2019).
Emotional regulation
  • Poor sleep increases amygdala reactivity 60%. More anxious, less focused, worse decisions.

SLEEP PROTOCOL — HUBERMAN + WALKER

7–9 hours

  • Non-negotiable. Athletes sleeping <7hrs are 1.7x more injury-prone (Walker, 2017).

Sunlight AM

  • Get 10 min morning sunlight within 30 min of waking. Sets circadian clock. (Huberman #1 tip).

Cool room 18–20°C

  • Core temp drop triggers melatonin. Cool room = faster sleep onset.

No screens 60 min

  • Blue light suppresses melatonin 50%. Read, stretch, breathe, or journal instead.

Same time ±30 min

  • Consistent bed/wake time — even weekends. Circadian rhythm is the master clock.

Nasal tape at night

  • Nestor: nasal breathing during sleep eliminates snoring, improves O₂ absorption.

4-7-8 in bed

  • Parasympathetic activation. 3–4 cycles. You’ll be asleep before cycle 4.

No caffeine after 2 PM

  • Caffeine half-life is 5–6 hours. Afternoon coffee fragments deep sleep.

PILLAR 2 — COLD EXPOSURE

55 RCTs confirm: cold water immersion reduces muscle damage, soreness, and inflammation (Wang et al., 2025).

Reduces DOMS
  • Delayed-onset muscle soreness significantly lower within 24–48 hours post-CWI.
Lowers CK levels
  • Creatine kinase (muscle damage marker) drops with 10–15 min at 11–15°C.
Dopamine +250%
  • Immersion at 14°C for extended duration increased dopamine 250% lasting 2–3 hours (Srámek et al., 2000).
Norepinephrine +530%
  • Massive norepinephrine release — improves focus, mood, alertness for hours after.
Activates brown fat
  • Cold triggers brown adipose tissue thermogenesis. Improves insulin sensitivity and metabolic health.
Mental resilience
  • Voluntary discomfort trains the prefrontal cortex to override the amygdala. Builds psychological toughness.

COLD EXPOSURE PROTOCOL — HUBERMAN

Temperature

  • 11–15°C (52–59°F). Uncomfortably cold but safe. Not extreme.

Duration

  • 10–15 min per session. Or shorter at colder temps.

Weekly total

  • 11 min minimum across 2–4 sessions (Huberman threshold).

Depth

  • Up to neck. Hands + feet submerged. Cold showers = second best.

Breathing

  • Slow nasal. Control the gasp. Coherent 5.5–5.5. Do NOT hyperventilate.

Timing

  • 6–8+ hours AFTER strength training. Fine immediately after surf technique sessions.

After, not before bed

  • Cold raises core temp for hours. Do it morning/afternoon, not before sleep.

Progression

  • Start warmer (15°C), shorter (2 min). Build tolerance over weeks. Like training.

PILLAR 3 — HYDRATION

2% dehydration = 20% cognitive decline + 25% physical performance drop.

  • Wittbrodt & Millard-Stafford (2018). You don’t feel thirsty in the ocean — that’s the trap.

Before session

  • 500ml water 30–45 min before. Add a pinch of sea salt for electrolytes.

During (if >90 min)

  • If accessible, sip water between sets. Coconut water works well in tropical conditions.

After session

  • 750ml–1L within first hour. Replace 150% of fluid lost (Sawka et al., 2007).

Electrolytes

  • Sodium, potassium, magnesium. Salt in water or electrolyte tabs. Not just plain water.

Urine check

  • Pale yellow = hydrated. Dark = drink now. Clear = you may be over-hydrating.

Alcohol warning

  • Alcohol is a diuretic and suppresses HGH by up to 70%. One drink = measurably worse recovery.

PILLAR 4 — NUTRITION TIMING

The 30–60 minute window after exercise is when your body is most receptive to nutrients.

Within 30 min

  • Protein + carbs. 20–40g protein + fast carbs. Smoothie, eggs + toast, protein shake + banana.

Protein

  • Repairs muscle. Target 1.6–2.2g per kg bodyweight per day for athletes (Morton et al., 2018).

Carbohydrates

  • Replenish glycogen. Essential after high-intensity sessions. Rice, oats, fruit, sweet potato.

Anti-inflammatory foods

  • Omega-3 (fish, avocado), turmeric, ginger, berries. Reduce systemic inflammation naturally.

Avoid post-session

  • Alcohol, excessive sugar, heavy fried food. These increase inflammation and delay repair.

Pre-session (2hrs before)

  • Complex carbs + moderate protein. Oatmeal, toast + peanut butter, rice + chicken.

PILLAR 5 — NERVOUS SYSTEM RESET

After intense sessions, your nervous system stays in sympathetic mode. You must consciously shift it back.

Coherent breathing

  • 5.5 sec in, 5.5 sec out. 5 minutes post-session. Activates vagus nerve. Shifts to parasympathetic.

Physiological sigh

  • Double inhale through nose + long exhale through mouth. The fastest single-breath reset (Huberman).

Legs up the wall

  • Lie on back, legs against wall. 5–10 min. Reduces cortisol, lowers heart rate, promotes venous return.

Non-Sleep Deep Rest (NSDR)

  • Huberman’s #1 recommendation. 10–20 min guided body scan while awake. Restores dopamine, reduces fatigue.

Yoga Nidra

  • Ancient version of NSDR. 20–30 min guided practice. Can replace 2 hours of lost sleep quality.

Sunlight exposure

  • 10 min afternoon sunlight. Sets circadian rhythm for better sleep tonight. Cortisol-lowering.

PILLAR 6 — SOFT TISSUE WORK

Fascia, muscles, and connective tissue need manual attention to maintain range of motion and prevent injury.

Foam rolling

  • Self-myofascial release. 1–2 min per muscle group. Focus on: quads, IT band, lats, thoracic spine. Reduces DOMS by 30% (Pearcey et al., 2015).

Massage

  • Professional massage 1–2x/month if accessible. Or partner-assisted post-session. Reduces cortisol, increases serotonin.

Stretching (post only)

  • Static stretching AFTER sessions only. Never before intense activity. 15–30 sec holds. Focus on shoulders, hips, ankles.

Lacrosse ball work

  • Targeted pressure on trigger points: feet (plantar fascia), glutes, shoulder blades. 60–90 sec per spot.

Fascia hydration

  • Fascia needs water to stay supple. Combine hydration + movement + rolling for best results.

Surfer priority zones

  • Shoulders (paddling), lower back (pop-up/rotation), hips (stance), ankles (board control), neck (looking up while paddling).

PILLAR 7 — ACTIVE RECOVERY

Light movement on rest days accelerates blood flow, clears metabolic waste, and maintains mobility without adding stress.

Walk (20–30 min)
  • Simplest recovery tool. Promotes blood flow, reduces cortisol, improves mood. Beach walk = bonus.
Light swimming
  • Low-impact, full-body movement. 15–20 min easy swim. Great for surfers — already in the ocean.
Yoga
  • Combines mobility, breathing, and nervous system reset. 20–30 min flow. Huberman: yoga is underrated for athletes.
Skateboarding (easy)
  • Balance and proprioception maintenance without water impact. Used by Sofía Mulanovich for active rest.
Mobility flow
  • Dynamic stretching circuit: hip circles, thoracic rotations, shoulder dislocates, ankle circles. 10 min.
Play
  • Non-structured movement: throw a frisbee, play with your kids, dance. Nieri: play reduces psychological burden.

RECOVERY DO vs DON’T

DO
  • Prioritize 8 hours of sleep above everything
  • Hydrate before, during, and after sessions
  • Eat protein + carbs within 30 min post-session
  • Use cold exposure strategically (not randomly)
  • Practice NSDR or Yoga Nidra on rest days
  • Foam roll priority zones 3–4x per week
DON’T
  • Sacrifice sleep for early sessions repeatedly
  • Drink alcohol after training (− 70% HGH)
  • Skip meals after intense sessions
  • Ice bath immediately after strength training
  • Scroll phone in bed (melatonin − 50%)
  • Train hard on consecutive days without recovery
