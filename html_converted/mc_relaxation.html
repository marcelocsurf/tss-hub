WHAT RELAXATION IS NOT

Relaxation is NOT being lazy. Relaxation is NOT being passive. Relaxation is NOT the absence of effort. Relaxation IS a trainable skill that regulates your nervous system, reduces unnecessary muscle tension, lowers anxiety, and creates the optimal internal state for learning and performing. The best athletes in the world are not the most tense. They are the most precisely relaxed.

3 / 17

WHY RELAXATION MATTERS FOR SURFING

Reduces cognitive anxiety
  • When your mind is calm, you read waves better, make faster decisions, and commit with more confidence.
Lowers somatic anxiety
  • Reduced heart rate, controlled breathing, and relaxed muscles allow fluid movement on the wave.
Improves technique execution
  • Tense muscles fight each other. Relaxed muscles cooperate. Your bottom turn is mechanically better when your body is not fighting itself.
Accelerates recovery
  • Relaxation activates the parasympathetic nervous system, which governs rest, repair, and energy restoration.
Improves sleep quality
  • Better sleep = better learning consolidation. Your brain processes today’s sessions during deep sleep.

THE NEUROSCIENCE

Your body has two operating modes:

Sympathetic

“Fight or Flight”

• Heart rate increases

• Muscles tense

• Cortisol floods the system

• Focus narrows (tunnel vision)

• Decision-making impaired

• Fine motor control decreases

Parasympathetic

“Rest and Digest”

• Heart rate decreases

• Muscles release tension

• Cortisol drops

• Awareness expands (peripheral)

• Decision-making improves

• Fine motor control increases

Relaxation training teaches you to shift from sympathetic to parasympathetic ON DEMAND.

WHAT THE RESEARCH SAYS

  • “Jacobson (1938)”
  • Developed Progressive Muscle Relaxation. Showed that systematically tensing and releasing muscles reduces overall nervous system arousal.
  • “Khir et al. (2024)”
  • Systematic review of 46 studies, 3,402+ adults across 16 countries: PMR is effective in reducing stress, anxiety, and depression. Combined with other techniques, efficacy increases further.
  • “10-study athlete review (2025)”
  • 7 out of 10 studies showed statistically significant reduction in competitive anxiety using PMR (p<0.05). One study showed anxiety scores dropping from 58.67 to 36.5 in just 4 weeks.
  • “Battaglini et al. (2022)”
  • Jacobson’s Progressive Relaxation effectively reduced cognitive anxiety, stress related to competition, fatigue, and overtraining in athletes.
  • “Cumming & Williams (2012)”
  • Relaxation combined with imagery produces greater neuromuscular pathway reinforcement than either technique alone.
  • “Bennett (2004)”
  • WCT elite surfers’ ideal performance state includes high relaxation, calm focus, and absence of unnecessary muscle tension.

3 PROVEN TECHNIQUES

Each addresses relaxation from a different angle. All are scientifically validated.

01

Progressive Muscle Relaxation
(PMR)
  • Jacobson, 1938

Tense and release each muscle group systematically. Teaches your body the DIFFERENCE between tension and relaxation. The most studied technique in sport psychology.

02

Diaphragmatic Breathing
(4-7-8 Method)
  • Weil, adapted

Inhale 4 seconds, hold 7, exhale 8. Activates the vagus nerve, which directly triggers the parasympathetic response. Works in under 60 seconds.

03

Body Scan
(Mindfulness-Based)
  • Kabat-Zinn, 1990

Slowly move attention through each body part without tensing. Notice sensation without judgment. Develops interoception — the ability to read your own body’s signals.

PMR — PROGRESSIVE MUSCLE RELAXATION

Jacobson (1938) — Tense 5 seconds, release slowly, feel the difference. Repeat 2–4 times per zone.

Biceps
Curl arm up, press bicep. Hold 5 sec. Release slowly.
Forearms
Close fist tight. Hold 5 sec. Open and release.
Shoulders
Raise shoulders to ears. Hold 5 sec. Drop and release.
Neck (forward)
Press chin to chest. Hold 5 sec. Return to neutral.
Neck (back)
Let head fall back gently. Hold 5 sec. Return.
Face
Squeeze all face muscles tight. Hold 5 sec. Release completely.
Back
Pull shoulder blades together. Hold 5 sec. Release.
Core
Tighten abdominals. Hold 5 sec. Release.
Legs
Press feet into floor, tense thighs. Hold 5 sec. Release.
Feet
Curl toes tight. Hold 5 sec. Release and feel warmth.

DIAPHRAGMATIC BREATHING — 4-7-8

The fastest way to activate your parasympathetic system. Works in under 60 seconds.

4

sec

INHALE
  • Through the nose.
  • Fill belly first, then chest.

7

sec

HOLD
  • Gently. No strain.
  • Let oxygen saturate.

8

sec

EXHALE
  • Through the mouth.
  • Slow, controlled, complete.

Repeat 4 cycles. Do this before every session, during land drills, or before sleep.

BODY SCAN — MINDFULNESS-BASED RELAXATION

1
Lie down or sit comfortably. Close your eyes. 3 deep breaths.
2
Bring attention to the top of your head. Notice sensation. Don’t change it.
3
Move slowly down: forehead, eyes, jaw, neck. Notice each area.
4
Shoulders, arms, hands. Feel warmth, tension, tingling — whatever is there.
5
Chest, belly, lower back. Notice your breath moving these areas.
6
Hips, thighs, knees, calves. Feel the weight of your legs.
7
Ankles, feet, toes. Notice the contact with the surface beneath you.
8
Now expand awareness to your whole body at once. Breathe. Hold this 30 seconds.
  • Develops interoception — the ability to read your own body’s signals. Critical for reading fatigue, fear, and readiness in the water.

WHEN TO USE EACH TECHNIQUE

Before a session

4-7-8 Breathing

  • Calms nerves, centers focus. 2 minutes on the beach before briefing.

During warm-up

Short PMR (hands, shoulders, jaw)

  • Releases unconscious tension before entering water.

After wipeout / fear spike

4-7-8 Breathing

  • Immediate parasympathetic activation. Regain composure.

Post-session

Full PMR or Body Scan

  • Accelerates physical and mental recovery.

Before sleep

Body Scan

  • Improves sleep quality. Brain consolidates learning during deep sleep.

Red Code / Plan B day

Full PMR + Visualization

  • Relaxation is the gateway to effective visualization (Nieri, Cumming & Williams).

GUIDED RELAXATION — SURF-SPECIFIC

Pre-Session Calm Down (5 minutes)

Sit on the sand. Board next to you. Close your eyes.

Three deep breaths. Inhale 4 seconds… hold 7… exhale 8. Repeat.

Feel the sand under you. Feel the warmth. Hear the ocean.

Tense your hands into fists. Hold 5 seconds. Release. Feel the difference.

Tense your shoulders up to your ears. Hold 5 seconds. Drop them. Feel them heavy.

Tighten your jaw. Hold 5 seconds. Release. Let your mouth hang slightly open.

Tense your whole body — everything at once. Hold 5 seconds. Release EVERYTHING.

Now breathe naturally. Scan your body from head to toes. Find any remaining tension. Breathe into it.

Set your intention: one clear objective for this session. See it. Feel it. You are ready.

Open your eyes. Stand up. Pick up your board. Walk toward the water.

TIPS & COMMON MISTAKES

DO: Practice when you’re NOT stressed
  • Train the skill in calm conditions so it’s automatic under pressure.
DO: Combine with visualization
  • Relaxation first, then imagery. The brain is more receptive when relaxed (Nieri, Cumming & Williams).
DO: Use breath as your anchor
  • When everything else fails, you always have your breath. 4-7-8 works anywhere.
DON’T: Force relaxation
  • Trying hard to relax creates more tension. Let it happen. Allow, don’t demand.
DON’T: Skip it because you feel fine
  • Relaxation is preventive, not reactive. The best time to practice is when you don’t ‘need’ it.
DON’T: Use only one technique
  • Different situations need different tools. Breathing for quick reset. PMR for deep release. Body scan for awareness.
