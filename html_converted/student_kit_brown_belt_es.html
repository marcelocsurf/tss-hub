SECTION 2 — YOUR SEQUENCES

▶

#18. Re-Mastery: Sequences #1-#17 at 8/10+

Every sequence from White through Purple. Every named expression. Every execution chain. You execute them at 8/10+ consistency across all conditions. Your bottom turns are deeper. Your projection more powerful. Your linking instinctive. You don't just DO these skills — you OWN them. When you paddle out, these movements are your default, not your aspiration.

#19. Aerial System: Ramp, Air, Landing

The first introduction to aerials. Three distinct parts: RAMP (find the section, generate speed), AIR (the rotation or grab), LANDING (stick it or demonstrate full commitment). This sequence teaches you the architecture of leaving the wave. Not every attempt will result in success — the goal is understanding the mechanics and committing to the launch.

#20. Combination Mastery: 3+ Maneuvers

Read the wave, chain 3+ maneuvers fluidly. No prescribed combinations here. The wave tells you what to do. Your job is to listen and respond. You link a bottom turn into a projection into a variation, or you flow a choke into a tapaloco into a snap. The sequence changes based on the section in front of you.

#21. Condition Adaptation: Every Break Type

Beach breaks, reef breaks, point breaks, river mouths. Small waves, large waves, mushy waves, hollow waves. You surf all of them effectively. You know what board to use. You adapt your approach to what the ocean gives you. Board selection is no longer a guess — it's a deliberate choice based on swell, wind, and tide.

#22. Competition Readiness & Session Maximization

Two paths forward. Competitive: ISA scoring, heat strategy, time management, pre-competition routine. You understand how judges score. You know when to wait and when to go. You have developed YOUR routine — predictable, repeatable, anxiety-reducing. Recreational: Maximize every session. Best positioning. Best wave selection. Best use of your time. You choose the path that fits your goals.

SECTION 3 — THE AERIAL SYSTEM

▶

Aerials are the signature expression of elite surfing. The system has three parts, and you will understand each deeply.

THE RAMP

Speed is everything. Find the section that's about to throw — the whitewash building, the pocket deepening. The steeper and faster, the higher you'll go. Speed 4 Paddling taught you how to generate power from flatwater. Now you need that same explosion on the wave face. Attack the ramp with violence. Generate momentum. When you launch, you launch HARD.

THE AIR

Once airborne, commit to the rotation or grab. Don't think mid-air — decide BEFORE you launch. You are committed to a specific movement: a 360 rotation (forward or reverse), a single grab (reaching for rail for style and control), or an alley-oop (reverse rotation — opposite to the wave's direction). Your body knows the pattern. Execute it.

THE LANDING

Spot your landing while still airborne. Absorb with bent knees. Recover posture immediately. You now have two standards: Competitive path — you MUST stick your landings. No credit for committed falls. Recreational path — demonstrate full power and commitment. A committed fall is better than a hesitant landing.

SECTION 6 — COMPETITION PATH (IF APPLICABLE)

▶

ISA SCORING

Judges award points for radical maneuvers executed in the critical section with power, speed, and flow. One powerful, committed maneuver beats three hesitant ones. Understand this. Understand that a 7-point maneuver is better than two 4-point maneuvers. Understand that execution matters more than quantity.

HEAT STRATEGY

Know the clock. Know what score you need. Know when you're winning and when you're chasing. Surf the wave, not the scoreboard. But understand the scoreboard so completely that you can ignore it and make correct decisions.

PRIORITY RULES

Closest to the peak has right of way. Know when to wait. Know when to go. Know when you're in priority and can take the next set wave. Respect the person on the wave ahead of you.

PRE-COMPETITION ROUTINE

Develop YOUR routine. Night before: hydrate, eat, sleep well, prepare gear, visualize. Morning of: eat, hydrate, warm up, review focus triggers, visualize execution chains. Predictable routine equals reduced anxiety. You don't figure out what to do at competition — you execute what you've rehearsed.

To compete seriously, complete ISA and WSL judge courses. Understand scoring from the inside. Watch how the best surfers approach heats. Learn the rules completely.
